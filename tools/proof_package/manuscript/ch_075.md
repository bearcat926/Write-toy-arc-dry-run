# Chapter 75: The Cost of a Moment

The Light That Bends held, but only barely. Dawnbreaker’s golden radiance and Lira’s elven script formed a fragile, shimmering lattice around the Obsidian Heart. But the artifact was not passive. It was a whirlpool of silent hunger, and the lattice was starting to fray. Veins of dark energy, like cracks in reality, began to spiderweb through the golden light.

“It’s too strong!” Kael grunted, his muscles screaming as he pushed against the Heart’s immense pressure. The Sanctum’s walls now seemed to be made of swirling, ink-dark smoke, the past and future bleeding into the present. “Lira, we can’t hold this!”

Lira’s face was pale, her brow slick with sweat, but her eyes burned with a scholar’s fierce certainty. “We are not meant to *hold* it, Kael. Only to give it a new direction.” Her voice was strained, yet unwavering. “You anchor the present. I will… offer it a thread of the past.”

She began to chant, her voice shifting from words to a pure, resonant frequency. The light from her hands turned from gold to a soft, silvery-blue—the colour of moonlight on ancient snow. Kael watched in horror as fine lines of white appeared in her dark hair, and a new depth, a sorrowful wisdom, etched itself around her eyes.

“Lira, no!”

“It is a memory,” she whispered, her voice echoing with the sacrifice. “A memory of the Heart’s making. A moment of… obedience.” She thrust her hands forward. The silvery light lanced toward the artifact, not as a barrier, but as an offering.

The Obsidian Heart shuddered violently. The chaotic, grasping hunger seemed to focus, to *pause*, as if listening. The temporal storm in the Sanctum stilled for a heartbeat. The walls solidified into ancient, carved stone.

In that profound silence, a new sound echoed: a slow, deliberate clap.

From the entrance of the Sanctum, stepping through a shadow that did not belong to the chamber, Commander Marcus emerged. His obsidian armour drank the light, and his smile was a thin, sharp line.

“How touching,” he said, his voice slithering across the quiet. “The elf burns her years to buy you a moment of clarity. A costly purchase.”

Lira slumped, catching herself on one knee, her breath coming in ragged gasps. The white streaks in her hair were stark and permanent.

Kael turned Dawnbreaker toward the new threat, the blade’s light now steady and pure against the Heart’s dormant dark. “This moment is ours, Shadowspawn.”

“Is it?” Marcus tilted his head. “You have contained it, warrior. But to *control* it requires a will forged in darkness.” He raised a hand, and tendrils of pure shadow coiled around his fingers, identical to the Heart’s veins. “That is what you lack.”
