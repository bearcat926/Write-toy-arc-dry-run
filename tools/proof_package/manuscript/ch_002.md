# Chapter 2: Echoes in the Amber

The whispers did not stop. They seeped from the walls of the hidden chamber, coiling around Kael’s mind like thorns. Before him, upon a black stone pedestal, pulsed the Obsidian Heart. It was a fist-sized sphere of living darkness, shot through with veins of molten amber that beat like a slow, captured heartbeat. It bathed the ancient carvings in a sickly, shifting light.

“Kael, step back.” Lira’s voice was a sharp whisper, her elven eyes wide with a mixture of academic wonder and primal fear. She clutched her rune-etched journal to her chest.

Kael couldn’t move. The artifact called to him, not in words, but in sensations: the ghostly touch of his father’s hand on his shoulder, the scent of the hearthfire from his lost home, the echo of a laughter silenced by a shadow’s blade. It promised to unwind the spool of time, just a little, just enough.

“The Prophecy fragment was right,” Lira murmured, her scholarly instincts warring with caution.
