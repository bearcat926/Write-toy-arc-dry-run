# Chapter 14: The Price of Passage

The coastal winds of Thalassia carried the salt-heavy scent of endings. Kael stood on the chalk cliffs, Dawnbreaker a dormant weight at his hip, staring at the grey expanse of the Serpent’s Maw strait. Lira’s map, now a brittle sheaf of parchment copied from the whispering stone, lay in his gauntlet. It showed a single, jagged island rising from the strait’s heart: the Isle of Whispers, where the Obsidian Heart was said to sleep in a temple of fused black glass.

“It is not a place one simply sails to,” Lira said softly, joining him. She pointed to the churning waters below. “The currents are treacherous, carved by old magic. The Thalassian Royal Fleet patrols it not out of defense, but as a ritual. To see the isle is considered an omen. To land there is madness.”

Kael’s hand tightened on the pommel of Dawnbreaker. The memory of the tomb, of ash and whispers, was a fresh scar. “We have the map.”

“We have a direction,” she corrected, her elven eyes sharp with concern. “We do not have a way. No captain in their right mind will take us, and any we force will betray us at the first reef.”

The argument was logical. It ignored the cold knot of duty in Kael’s gut, the weight of the Sunstrider oath pressing down on his shoulders. He thought of Marcus’s face in the moment before the light took him—not just an enemy commander, but a man claiming to save the world. “The prophecy says three deaths before the heart wakes.”

“It spoke of sacrifices,” Lira whispered, her earlier academic fervor now tempered with dread. “Not murders.”

A horn blew from the port town below, a deep, mournful note that echoed off the cliffs. Soldiers in the sea-green and silver of Thalassia were mustering on the docks, their banners snapping in the wind. Among them, a different banner flew: a black circle on a field of midnight blue. The Shadow Council. They had arrived first, and by sea.
