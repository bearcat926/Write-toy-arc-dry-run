# Chapter 85: The Shattered Vessel

Kael watched the hairline fracture race across the Obsidian Heart, a dark vein pulsing with sickly, inverted light. It wasn’t breaking; it was *unsealing*. The silence that followed Dawnbreaker’s strike was not peace, but the held breath before a scream.

“Kael, the temporal bleed,” Lira’s voice was a strained whisper. She stood frozen, her hand outstretched as if to ward off the very air. Where her fingers pointed, motes of ancient dust were reassembling into the ghost of a fallen flower, then crumbling to ash, then reassembling again—a frantic, trapped loop.

His body protested as he forced himself upright. The wound in his side, inflicted by a blade of concentrated time, didn’t bleed; it felt cold, a hollow where his own heat, his own *present*, was being siphoned. Dawnbreaker’s light was a guttering candle in his fist.

“The Heart is failing,” Kael grunted, the truth of it settling in his bones. It wasn’t a tool anymore. It was a wounded god, lashing out. The decay wasn’t just in the stone anymore; it was seeping into the fabric of the Sanctum itself, threatening to unravel the very moment they occupied.

Marcus’s body was gone, not dissolved but *unstitched* from time, leaving only the echo of a final, silent howl. The victory felt like ash. This was their doing. The artifact, now their responsibility.
