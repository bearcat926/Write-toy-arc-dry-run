# Chapter 92: The Unmaking Price

The name hung in the dead air between them, a poisoned dart. *Marcus.* Not a title, not a rank, but a name. It resonated in the marrow of Kael’s bones, pulled from the blood-memory the Heart had forced into him.

The Shadow Council commander’s smile was a thin, cold thing. “You remember. Good. That will save tedious explanations.” He took a slow step forward, his boots making no sound on the fractured stone. The shadows around him deepened, coiling like smoke. “The Heart shows you what it needs you to see. It called you here, Kael Sunstrider. As it called me, long ago.”

“Liar,” Kael snarled, forcing himself to his feet. Dawnbreaker’s light was a wavering gold shroud around him, offering warmth that couldn’t reach his chilled core. The weapon felt less like a tool and more like a leash, connecting him to the arrhythmic pulse of the Obsidian Heart. Each throb was a whisper of disintegration. “You serve a council that wants to unravel the world.”

Marcus’s gaze flickered to Lira, who had risen silently, her hand near the satchel holding her lenses and notes. “The elf sees the truth in fragments. You feel it in your blood. The Council doesn’t want to unravel the world, warrior. We want to *control* the unraveling. To direct the flow of moments.” He gestured toward the Heart. “This broken god-tool is the only means.”

“It’s a curse,” Lira said, her voice tight with scholarly conviction and personal fear. “Its use erodes the user. It consumes moments to fuel itself. Valdris didn’t fall to an army. It was *unmade* by this.”

“Unmade and remade,” Marcus corrected gently, his eyes returning to Kael. His intensity was terrifying, focused not on the artifact, but on the man. “Why do you think the Sunstrider oath binds you to stand against the dark? Your clan’s founder, Aleron, was there at the first breach. He felt what you feel now. A resonance. The Heart is attuned to bloodlines that have brushed against its touch. It needs a key to turn within its lock. It needs a *witness*
