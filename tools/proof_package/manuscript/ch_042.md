# Chapter 42: The Echoing Fall

Kael hit the ground with a jolt that rattled his teeth. Not the black glass plain, but the cold, gritty ice of the glacier. He was on his hands and knees, gasping. The air burned his lungs, shockingly ordinary.

“Kael!” Lira was beside him instantly, her hands hovering over the frost that now entirely sheathed his arms and was climbing his neck. The patterns were different. No longer just maps, but moving images—a village in flames, a falling king, a star going dark. Temporal echoes, trapped in ice.

“What did you see?” Her voice was tight with fear and scholarly hunger.

“The Heart,” Kael rasped, his own breath pluming white. “It’s not in Valdris, or Nordmark. It’s… between. The Vein of Hours. A place where all the broken moments pool.” He remembered the sensation of falling through centuries, not down, but *sideways*. The cost was becoming clear: each use didn’t just drain him, it pulled fragments of other times into his own existence, freezing them onto his skin and into his blood.

Lira’s eyes widened. She recognized the term. “The Serpent’s Coil. It’s a myth. A fold in the world where time is said to bleed.” She looked at his arm, where a fleeting vision of a silver-haired woman screaming was now etched in frost. “This isn’t a map. It’s a wound.”

A second horn blast, closer. The crunch of boots on snow announced Marcus’s vanguard cresting the nearby ridge. There was no time.

“We can’t run,” Lira stated, her voice dropping to a whisper. Her hand went to the satchel at her hip. “Kael. The frost… it’s showing you *when*, not just *where*.”

He understood. The frozen images were echoes of moments the Obsidian Heart had already touched. The village burned because someone had tried to save it and failed. The king fell because an hour was stolen. The cost wasn’t just life, it was the unraveling of cause and effect.

Marcus’s troops were forming a line, black cloaks against the white. He stood at their fore, his own obsidian blade resting on his shoulder, smiling a thin, cold smile. He thought he was herding them.

Kael pushed himself to his feet, the frost crackling. Dawnbreaker felt heavy, its golden light dimmed by the temporal frost leaching from him. But he saw now. The Heart wasn’t a prize to be won, but a cancer to be cut out. And the Vein of Hours was its lair.
