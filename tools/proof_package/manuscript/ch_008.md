# Chapter 8: The Echo of a Fallen Sword

The tunnel breathed dust. Kael sat on a crumbled block of stone, his back against the cold wall, staring at the space where Marcus had been. The body was gone. Not buried, not dissolved by arcane backlash—simply gone. In its place lay only scattered ash and the shattered remains of a shadow-steel gauntlet.

Lira’s light-stone cast sharp, white beams into the gloom, its glow seeming harsh and unwelcome after the amber pulses of the Heart. “The temporal flux,” she murmured, her fingers brushing the ash without touching it. “When you touched the Heart… what did it show you, Kael? Specifically.”

He didn’t answer at first. The whispers from the vial in his pouch had become a low, seductive murmur, like the promise of sleep after a long battle. It showed me his death, he wanted to say. It showed me victory. But the memory was already fraying at the edges, replaced by a new, more terrible certainty. The Heart hadn’t shown him the future. It had shown him *a* future. One he had then severed.

“His sword,” Kael said, his voice rough. “I saw him swing his sword. Not at me. At the supports. He was going to bring the tunnel down on all of us.”

Lira’s eyes, sharp as a hawk’s, fixed on him. “You used the vial. You bent the moment.”

“I ended it,” he corrected, a tremor of awe and horror in his tone. “I didn’t just foresee. I… *edited*.”
