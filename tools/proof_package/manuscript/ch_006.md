# Chapter 6: Ashes on the Wind

The silence in the collapsed tunnel was heavier than the stone that had fallen. Kael stood over Marcus’s body, not with the triumph of a warrior, but with the hollow shock of a man who had stepped off a cliff. Dawnbreaker, its celestial light dimmed, hung loosely in his grip. The shadow-tendrils that had erupted from his heart in that blinding moment had dissolved like smoke, leaving only the acrid scent of ozone and a deep, freezing cold in his core.

“What… what was that?” Lira’s voice was a whisper, her usual scholarly calm fractured. She hadn’t seen the whispers manifest as physical force before.

Kael couldn’t answer. He stared at the commander of the Shadow Council, whose lifeless eyes reflected the faint ambient glow of the Heart’s residual magic. It wasn’t a clean kill. It was an execution by a power Kael didn’t understand and hadn’t chosen. The whispers were silent now, sated, leaving a terrible void.

“The Heart,” he finally managed, his voice raw. “It… responded to my rage.” He looked at his hands, half-expecting to see them wreathed in black fire. They were just his hands, calloused and steady, but he felt like a stranger wearing them.

Lira stepped closer, her elven senses scanning not just the darkness ahead, but him. “It’s reacting to you, Kael. Not just your proximity. Your emotions. It’s feeding, and growing stronger.” She gestured to the vial of amber at his belt, which now pulsed with a faint, constant light. “The artifact and its key are resonating.”

A new tremor shook the tunnel, more violent than the last. Dust rained from the ceiling. The collapse they’d triggered was destabilizing the entire passage.

“We have to move,” Lira said, her analytical mind taking over the panic. “His people will come for him. And the structure won’t hold.”

Kael sheathed Dawnbreaker, the gesture feeling like a betrayal. He knelt, not to mourn Marcus, but to search him. His fingers found a heavy, iron-bound ledger inside the commander’s tunic, and a crystalline shard that hummed with a familiar, dark energy—a sliver of the Obsidian Heart’s power, likely used to command the shadows.

He pocketed both, the ledger a potential map to the Shadow Council’s plans, the shard a dangerous tool. As he stood, his gaze met Lira’s. The doubt in her eyes mirrored his own horror.
