# Chapter 38: The Borrowed Dawn

The fire in the brazier sputtered, casting jagged shadows that danced with the frost-ferns on the canvas. Lira had given up on her healing chants. She now sat with her back straight, unrolling the star-chart she’d carried from Thalassia, though her eyes were on Kael.

“We cannot stay,” she said, her voice soft but firm. “The energy signature of the Heart’s withdrawal is a beacon. Any with magical sight in a hundred leagues will have felt it. Marcus will be coming.”

Kael didn’t look up. The patterns on the canvas were moving again, slowly, not as creeping frost, but as a slow, deliberate tide of blue-white light, forming a map. Not of Nordmark, but of a place he had only seen in fragments of a time not his own—the crumbling spires of Valdris, before the first war of the kingdoms.

“He saw it,” Kael murmured. “The echo. He saw what I saw. The cost.”

“The cost for whom?” Lira pressed, crawling closer. The chart lay forgotten between them.

“The one who takes,” Kael said, his voice hollow. He finally turned to look at her, and Lira drew a sharp breath. His eyes, once the warm brown of sun-baked clay, now held flecks of gold around the pupils, bright and unsettling as frozen sparks. “But also for the world it’s taken from. A moment is stolen. A person. A choice. The timeline… it doesn’t just heal. It scars. Those moments… they have to go somewhere.”
