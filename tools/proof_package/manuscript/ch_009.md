# Chapter 9: The Shattered Veil

The ash was cold. Kael let the last grains sift through his fingers, watching them settle on the cracked flagstones where Marcus had fallen. Dawnbreaker lay across his knees, its light a feeble, guttering candle against the oppressive gloom of the tomb. The silence was no longer hollow; it was charged, pregnant with the impossible.

"It wasn't decay," Lira said, her voice barely a whisper. She knelt by the scorch mark, her long fingers tracing patterns only she could see in the discolouration. "And it wasn't disintegration. The energy signature is... torn. Fractured."

Kael didn't look up. "He's gone, Lira."

"No." Her voice gained a sharp, scholarly edge. "He's *displaced*. I've seen references in fragmented Thalassian chronicles—the Obsidian Heart doesn't just manipulate time, Kael.
