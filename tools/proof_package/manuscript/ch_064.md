# Chapter 64: The Price of the Path

The shard’s certainty was a cage of light in the endless dark. Kael’s body obeyed it, climbing a tilted slab of stone slick with meltwater, his injured shoulder screaming in protest. The knowledge told him the structural fault lay three hands to the left, that the weight distribution was safe for one more passage. It did not tell him how to quiet the tremor in his hands.

“Kael.” Lira’s voice was thin, strained from behind him. “Your eyes… they’re not focusing.”

He couldn’t look back. Looking back meant hesitating, and the path was a relentless current. “I’m fine. The exit is close. Twenty more meters.”

He could feel it—a subtle change in air pressure, a faint lessening of the oppressive cold that meant a connection to the outside world. The shard thrummed with approval, a warm pulse against his palm.

“Stop.” Lira’s command was sharp, echoed by the scrape of her boots on stone. “For one moment, stop.”

He turned, the movement jerky. In the faint luminescence from the ice, her face was a pale oval of concern. “We can’t. The integrity—”
