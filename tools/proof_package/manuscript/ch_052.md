# Chapter 52: The Frost-Witch’s Choice

The hum became a language Kael could almost parse. It wasn't sound, but the vibration of something ancient and cold resonating in the marrow of his bones, in the crystalline structure of the glacier itself. It spoke of a place—not with words, but with a direction. Nordmark. The northern heart of frost and silence. The hum was not a call; it was a *pull*, like the tide obeying a moon he could not see.

“The Obsidian Heart,” Lira whispered, her breath a white cloud in the frigid air. She had felt the shift in him, a subtle reorientation, as if he were a lodestone. “It’s waking for you. Or… you are waking to it.”

Kael opened his eyes. The silver echoes from Lira’s healing were gone, but something new lingered in his gaze—a depth, as if he were seeing the cave walls not as they were, but as they had been and could be. “It doesn’t want me,” he said, his voice rough. “It wants the scar. The one in time it needs to fill.”

“The prophecy,” Lira said, not a question. Her scholar’s mind raced, connecting fragments from the Valdris Archives and the frost-whispers. “’When the unscarred hand reaches for the heart of shadow, the world’s wounds shall be made anew.’ But you are not unscarred, Kael. You are a map of them.”

“Maybe that’s the point.” He ran a thumb over Dawnbreaker’s hilt, feeling the hum echo in the blade. “A warrior’s scars are a kind of time, Lira. They mark the past. This thing… it wants to rewrite the map.”

He stood, the hum settling into a low thrum in his chest. It led north. Always north. But a different path than they’d planned, one that skirted the mountain passes and plunged into the ancient, cursed Frostfang Fells—a place even Nordmark’s own jarls avoided.
