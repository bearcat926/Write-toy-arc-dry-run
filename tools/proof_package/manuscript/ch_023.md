# Chapter 23: The Bleeding of Seconds

The vision shattered, but its residue remained. Kael was back on his knees in the glacial chamber, one hand still outstretched toward the Obsidian Heart. The air tasted of ozone and ash, a phantom of the burned sun he’d witnessed. A single, wet warmth traced a path from his nose to his lip. He touched his face, and his fingers came away crimson.

“Kael! Your nose is bleeding,” Lira whispered, her voice tight. She didn’t move closer, her eyes fixed on the artifact. It still pulsed with that deep, hungry rhythm, but now, tiny fractures like spiderwebs of light were spreading through the ice beneath it, radiating outward.

“The sun… I saw it die,” Kael rasped, pushing himself to his feet. His body felt strangely light, as if a weight had been removed, yet simultaneously fragile. “And I was there. I felt the heat.”

“The Heart shows possibilities, threads of time,” Lira said, her scholarly tone warring with awe and fear. “What you saw was a *potential* future. One of many.”

“It felt absolute.” The memory of incandescent heat and screaming skies was seared into his mind, a counterpoint to the tomb-like cold around them. He looked at his hands, half-expecting them to be sunburnt. They were pale, trembling.

A soft, rhythmic *crack* drew their attention. One of the spreading fractures in the ice floor had reached the base of a glacial pillar. Where it touched, the ice didn’t melt; it simply *aged*, becoming cloudy and brittle in a spreading stain. A second later, that section of the pillar collapsed into fine, powdered snow, revealing the raw, dark rock beneath.

“It’s affecting the structure,” Lira breathed, taking a step back. “The temporal energy is bleeding out, accelerating decay.”

“The cost,” Kael muttered, understanding dawning. The prophecy spoke of a terrible price
