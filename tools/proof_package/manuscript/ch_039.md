# Chapter 39: A Shard of Tomorrow

The horn blast was distant, but its resonance vibrated in Kael’s frost-laced bones. Marcus. The Shadow Council had not waited for the glacier to settle.

“We have to move,” Lira said, already packing the chart. Her efficiency was a thin veil over her fear; her hands trembled as she folded the parchment.

Kael nodded, his movements slow, deliberate. The emptiness inside him had begun to shift, not filling, but hardening. It was a cold, crystalline thing now, a mirror to the frost that crept along his skin. He shouldered his pack, his gaze falling on the small, dark pouch Lira had placed beside him. The Obsidian Heart shard.

He picked it up. It was no larger than a sparrow’s egg, but its weight was immense, a sliver of midnight that drank the firelight. The moment his fingers closed around it, the cold in his marrow *pulled* toward it. A silent, hungry current.

“Kael, don’t,” Lira warned, her voice sharp.

He ignored her. He needed to know. He needed a direction, any direction. He closed his eyes, focusing not on the void in his chest, but on the shard. *Show me. Where do we go? Where does the Heart lie?*

Images, fractured and cold, seared behind his eyelids. Not a place, but a moment: a sun-drenched plaza in Valdris, the royal palace gleaming white. A child’s laughter echoing off marble. Then the light curdled, shadows lengthening unnaturally across the stones, and the laughter twisted into a scream. He felt a pull, not toward the palace, but beneath it. To a place where the stone was old and the silence was absolute. And he felt Marcus’s presence, not chasing, but waiting. Waiting there.

He gasped, dropping the shard. It fell into the ashes of the fire, pulsing with a faint, dark light.

“What did you see?” Lira asked, her scholar’s curiosity warring with her fear.
