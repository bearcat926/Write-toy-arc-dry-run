# Chapter 61: The Shard's Allure

The whisper was no longer a whisper. It was a pulse in Kael’s veins, a second heartbeat synced to the shard of obsidian clutched in his fist. It showed him things not as images, but as *knowings*. He *knew* the path ahead was stable. He *knew* the groaning ceiling of ice would hold for another minute. And he *knew*, with a certainty that chilled him deeper than the glacial tomb, that Lira was alive. Not just alive, but *there*.

He moved through a tunnel not of his making. The glacier, in its final throes, had spat out passages like a dragon gasping air. The shard in his hand was a lodestone, pulling him through the labyrinth. Its whispers were no longer seductive promises of power; they were practical, urgent. *Step left. The stone is rotten. Duck. Now.*

It was terrifying, this intimate guidance from an artifact of ruin. But it was saving his life.

He emerged into a vast, vaulted chamber where the glacier’s heart had been. The walls were ribbed with the same obsidian veins, now dark and still. In the center, half-buried in a drift of powdered ice, was a cocoon of pale, shimmering light. Lira’s ward.

Kael scrambled down the slope, the shard’s guidance forgotten. “Lira!” His voice was a raw scrape.

The light flickered and dissolved. Lira was curled within, shivering violently, her lips blue. Her eyes, glazed with cold, focused on him with immense effort. “Kael… you felt it. The link.”
