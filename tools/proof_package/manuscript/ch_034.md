# Chapter 34: The Sunstrider's Shadow

The world returned in shards: the bite of frozen flagstones against his knees, the phantom taste of blood and ozone, and a profound, aching emptiness where his heart should have been. Kael was not dead, but the man who had faced the Obsidian Heart was. In his place was something quieter, something that had glimpsed the architecture of moments and now felt unstrung from them.

Lira’s hands were on his shoulders, her touch a fragile anchor. “Kael, breathe. You are here.”

He looked up. The glacial chamber was gone. They knelt in the courtyard of Frosthold, under a bruised twilight sky. Snowflakes melted in Lira’s hair. The Valdrian banner, the sunburst of his clan, hung limp on its pole, spattered with mud. The battle was over, but this was not victory.

“He took it,” Kael whispered, his voice raw. “Marcus… he has the Heart.”

Lira’s elven eyes, usually bright with scholarly curiosity, were dark with a sorrow that mirrored his own. “He did. But what you did, Kael… you didn’t just use it. You *argued* with time itself. The cost…” Her fingers tightened. “I felt it in your spirit-light. You didn’t just spend strength. You spent *continuity*.”

He didn’t understand, not fully, but he felt the truth of it. Memories felt thin, like overused parchment. The faces of his clansmen, once bright, seemed slightly out of focus. He was a warrior who could no longer trust his own past.

A courier, breathless and frost-bitten, stumbled into the courtyard. “My lords! The Shadow Council banners have been sighted beyond the Blackwood. They move not for Eryndor, but for the Temporal Caverns. Commander Marcus… he travels with a shroud of unnatural light.”

Kael’s hands curled into fists on his knees. The emptiness inside him flickered with a cold, new flame. Not the fierce sun-anger of his clan, but something sharper. Purpose.

“He’s not hiding,” Kael said, the realization cutting through the fog of his loss. “He’s testing it. The Heart’s power. He’ll try to… alter something. Something foundational.”
