# Chapter 1: The Price of a Moment

The air in the Sunken Temple of Valdris tasted of dust and old magic. Kael braced his shoulder against the crumbling stone door, muscles straining, the sigil of the Sunstrider clan— an eagle clutching a sunburst—standing stark white on his leather armor. Sweat beaded on his brow, not just from effort, but from the low, thrumming pulse that emanated from the chamber beyond. It was a feeling like a second heartbeat, sick and invasive against his own.

“It’s not just locked, it’s waiting,” murmured Lira, her elven fingers tracing the archway’s fading runes. Her eyes, the color of deep twilight, were wide. “The containment spells are failing. It knows we’re here.”

“It’s an artifact, Lira. Not a beast,” Kael grunted, though a chill traced his spine. The Obsidian Heart. For ten years, since the prophecy had scorched itself into the minds of the clan’s seers, this was the only thing that mattered. A relic that could unmake a single moment in time. To prevent the Fall of Eryndor. To save his father.

With a final heave, the door ground open. Dust motes danced in the shaft of light from the collapsed ceiling, illuminating a central dais. There it rested: a fist-sized shard of polished obsidian, veined with a sickly, pulsating violet light. It looked deceptively small to be the fulcrum upon which history balanced.

Kael stepped forward, Dawnbreaker, his ancestral sword, humming quietly in its scabbard. The artifact’s pulse quickened, syncing with his stride. Images flashed at the edges of his mind—a childhood laugh, the scent of his mother’s garden, the exact angle of the sun on the day his father left for Eryndor. It was offering him a catalog of moments. A horrifying selection.

“Kael, stop.” Lira’s voice was sharp, her hand gripping his arm. Her scholarly composure had fractured. “The texts are clear. It doesn’t *give* you a moment back. It *exchanges* one. A moment for a moment. A joy for a sorrow. You could lose the memory of her smile to prevent his death. You could trade his death for your own birth. The cost is… unpredictable. Contaminated.”

He shook her off, his own heart hammering against the artifact’s rhythm. “I know the cost. A Sunstrider always pays.” He reached out, his calloused fingers hovering just above the cold, glassy surface. The violet veins within flared, and in their light, he didn’t see his own reflection.
