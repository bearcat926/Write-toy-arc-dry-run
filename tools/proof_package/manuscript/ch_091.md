# Chapter 91: The Blood-Memory

The golden light of Dawnbreaker steadied, not because Kael controlled it, but because the artifact’s chaotic pulse momentarily synchronized with his own heartbeat. The synchronization was a violation. He could feel the Heart not as an object, but as a stolen pulse, a rhythm siphoned from the fabric of what was.

“It is learning you,” Lira whispered, her voice hoarse. She hadn’t moved from the floor, her scholarly robes pooling around her like spilled ink. “The Makers’ device. It doesn’t just show time. It… experiences it through a conduit. You are that conduit now.”

Kael’s hand, resting on Dawnbreaker’s hilt, trembled. A new vision bloomed, sharper than the rest. It wasn’t Valdris dissolving. It was the moment before. He saw a figure in a silvered helm standing before the Heart—whole, on its obsidian pedestal. The figure’s gauntleted hand reached out, and as their fingers brushed its surface, the helm cracked down the center, revealing a scarred face he knew. His own face. Older, lined with a grief he had not yet earned.

The vision collapsed. The blood was real, pooling beneath his left hand. He’d clenched it so tight the nails had broken the skin.

“The cost,” Kael gritted out, looking at the crimson drops on the stone. “When it showed me Valdris’s fall… it wasn’t a memory. It was a payment. My blood to feed the vision.”

Lira crawled forward, her elven eyes wide with a scholar’s horrified awe. “A blood-memory. The texts spoke of it. A sacrifice of potential life-force to anchor a temporal echo. Kael, every time you use its power, even passively, you are giving up moments of your own future.”

He looked at the fractured Heart. It seemed to lean toward him, a dark, hungry stone. “So I can’t wield it. I can’t even hold it.”

“No,” she said, her tone shifting from awe to grim resolution. “But Marcus can. And he will not bleed. The Shadow Council has blood to spare.” She pointed. A faint, dark stain was spreading on the floor near the chamber’s archway—a drop of oil, a smear of black wax. A silent, sorcerous marker. “He’s closer than we thought. This place is weakening, folding under the Heart’s strain. He’s bypassing our wards.”
