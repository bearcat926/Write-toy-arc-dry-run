# Chapter 22: The Price of Knowing

The vision did not come gently. It slammed into Kael’s consciousness the instant his fingers brushed the warm, unyielding surface of the Heart. One moment he was in the glacial chamber, Lira’s gasp echoing behind him; the next, he was elsewhere.

He saw the sun setting over the walls of Eryndor, but the light was wrong—it bled upwards, a sickly crimson. Marcus stood upon the ramparts, not in his dark commander’s armor, but in the regal silks of a king. Below him, the city burned, and from the flames rose… nothing. An absence, a consuming void that unraveled reality like threads from a tapestry. And pinned to the stones by a spear of shadow, her lifeblood freezing as it touched the cobblestones, was Lira. Her eyes found his across the impossible distance, filled not with pain, but with a terrible, knowing pity.

Kael wrenched his hand back with a cry, staggering. The chamber snapped back into focus—the blue ice, the hovering obsidian, Lira’s frightened face.

“Kael! What did you see?” She rushed to his side, her scholar’s curiosity warring with concern.

He couldn’t speak. He could still feel the phantom grief, a blade twisting in his gut. The prophecy, the Heart, Marcus—it was all converging on a single, catastrophic point. And Lira was at its center.

“It… showed me an end,” he managed, his voice rough. “A possible end.”

“The Heart manipulates time,” Lira whispered, her eyes wide with dawning, academic horror. “It doesn’t just store power. It shows… possibilities. Probabilities. What you saw is the path we are most likely to tread without intervention.”

A low, resonant hum vibrated from the Heart, and the pulsing veins of deeper black quickened. The air, already frigid, grew sharp with the scent of ozone and distant thunder.

“We need to take it,” Lira said, her tone shifting from scholar to strategist. “We cannot leave it here. Marcus will be—”
