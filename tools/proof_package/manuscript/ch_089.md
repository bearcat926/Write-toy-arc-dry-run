# Chapter 89: The Bleeding Hour

Kael didn’t feel the impact of his knees hitting the stone floor. He felt *time* hit him—not as a river, but as a tide of broken glass, each shard a moment that sliced as it passed. Dawnbreaker lay beside him, its golden light flickering erratically, like a candle in a gale. The whispers were gone. In their place was a horrifying clarity: he could see the minutes bleeding from the chamber walls, staining the air with a faint, silvery shimmer.

“Kael, your nose.” Lira’s voice was tight with alarm. She was at his side, her elven senses likely screaming from the temporal distortion. He brought a hand to his face; it came away slick with blood, dark in the unstable light.

“The Heart… it’s not just open,” he rasped, the words tasting of ozone and old dust. “It’s *hemorrhaging*.” He could feel it now, a deep, syncopated pulse in the world’s fabric, tugging at the edges of his own moments. A memory of his mother’s face flickered, then twisted, aging decades in a heartbeat before snapping back. He flinched.

“The fracture is a wound in causality,” Lira breathed, her eyes wide as she looked from the cracked obsidian orb to his bleeding face. “And you’re tied to it. The strike that cracked it… your life force is part of the seal’s broken equation.”

A shadow detached itself from the deeper gloom beyond the Heart’s dais. Marcus. The Shadow Council commander moved with a predator’s silence, his cloak blending with the unnatural dark. His face was a mask of grim triumph.

“Ah,” Marcus said, his voice a low curl of satisfaction. “The great warrior Sunstrider. I came to claim the Heart. But it seems you’ve already begun to claim a piece of it for yourself.” He gestured negligently at the air around Kael. “I can see the bleed. You’re a walking paradox, warrior. A man whose personal timeline is becoming… porous.”

Kael forced himself to his feet, snatching up Dawnbreaker. The sword felt heavier, its light sluggish. “I am the guardian of this seal, Marcus. You will not touch it.”

“The seal is already broken,” Marcus countered, taking a step forward. The shadows at his feet coiled like serpents. “And I don’t need the whole artifact. A fragment, suffused with a warrior’s unraveling life, will serve the Council’s purposes just as well. You’re not its keeper anymore. You’re its *offering*.”
