# Chapter 69: The Weight of a Moment

The shard’s command was a hook in his soul. Kael’s fingers, inches from the Obsidian Heart, trembled not with weakness but with the strain of resistance. The knowledge it offered was intoxicating—a map of every second in the Sanctum’s history, the structural stress points in the very air, the precise location of every shadow. But behind that data flowed a colder current: the Heart’s own hunger. It wanted to be held. To be used. It promised the power to mend the fractured path, to unwrite the failures that had led them here, buried under the weight of a dead city.

*Kael!* Lira’s voice, sharp with alarm, cut through the mental haze. He felt her hand grip his good shoulder, a tangible anchor in the tide of silent imperative. “Your eyes… they’ve gone glassy. Whatever it’s telling you, it isn’t truth. It’s a lure.”

He wanted to answer, to reassure her that it was just knowledge, just a tool. But the shard pulsed, and for a horrifying instant, he saw not Lira’s worried face but a vision *through* her eyes: himself, eyes blazing with unnatural light, reaching for the Heart as Dawnbreaker fell, forgotten, to the floor. A future. A possible future. The Heart showed him the consequence of its own use—the viewer becomes the vessel, their timeline pliable clay.

The cost wasn’t something paid later. It was paid in the losing of oneself, moment by moment.

With a roar that was part fury, part desperate prayer, Kael wrenched his will from the shard’s silent screams. He didn’t pull his hand back; instead, he closed his fist and slammed it down not upon the Heart, but upon the stone pedestal beside it. Pain exploded in his knuckles, a clean, sharp agony that was *his*.

“I am not your clay,” he gasped, the words tearing from his throat. He turned to Lira, his vision clearing, the shard’s whispers receding to a frustrated hum. “We… we cannot take it. To wield it is to be unmade by it.”
