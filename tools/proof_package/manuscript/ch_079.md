# Chapter 79: The Fractured Hour

The Valdrian Sanctum no longer obeyed the laws of time or space. Marcus stood at its center, a statue of living obsidian and malice, and the world around him began to *creak*. Not with sound, but with distortion. A pillar to Kael’s left aged a millennium in a heartbeat, crumbling to dust before reforming into polished marble. The scent of ozone and ancient parchment warred with the stench of temporal decay.

Kael raised Dawnbreaker, but the familiar, righteous warmth of the blade was dimmed, hesitant. It was like trying to kindle a fire in a hurricane of ghosts.

“Do you see, Sunstrider?” Marcus’s voice was a chorus, layered with echoes of the man he’d been and the entity he’d become. The obsidian growth pulsed. “You bring a candle to a black hole. You fight *moments*. I hold *continuity*.”

Lira moved to Kael’s side, her hands shaking, not with fear, but with frantic calculation. “His timeline is fractured, Kael! He’s not just here—he’s momentarily *everywhen* the Heart has touched! The lattice we wove didn’t contain it; it gave it… focus.”

Kael understood then. This wasn’t a battle against a stronger foe. It was a battle against a principle. To strike was to risk erasing a second that might unravel your own birth. His warrior’s certainty, the bedrock of his being, began to fracture.

*What is my swing against eternity?*

A memory, unbidden, sliced through his doubt. His father’s words, spoken in the forge’s glow. “Steel does not fear the river, Kael. It does not fight the current. It finds the one point where the flow is weakest, and it *holds*.” The memory was so vivid it burned, and he realized with a jolt that the Heart’s power was dredging his past, throwing it at him as a weapon.

But it wasn’t just a weapon. It was a clue.

Lira saw the shift in his eyes. “What is it?”

“He’s not unified,” Kael breathed, his voice low. “Lira, you said it yourself. Fractured. He’s trying to be every moment at once. That’s not a strength. It’s a fatal distraction.”

The obsidian growth on Marcus’s chest pulsed, and the floor beneath Kael’s feet suddenly aged, turning to soft, rotten wood. He leapt back, Dawnbreaker flashing not at Marcus, but at the floor itself, shearing away the affected section with a precise, cleansing strike.
