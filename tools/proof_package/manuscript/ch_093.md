# Chapter 93: The Commander's Vow

The name ‘Marcus’ did something to the air in the Chamber of Echoes. It thickened, became charged with a different kind of history—one of personal betrayal, not just cosmic dread. Kael, still on his knees, felt the blood-memory twist. It wasn’t just a face anymore. It was a memory of a younger man, laughing in a sun-drenched courtyard, a lieutenant’s insignia bright on his collar, shouting a warning to a friend. A friend whose face was now obscured by the visor of the Shadow Council commander.

“You remember.” Marcus’s voice was quiet, devoid of its earlier mockery. He stepped fully into the pulsing light of the Heart, and Kael saw the truth etched in the lines around his eyes. This wasn’t a conqueror; it was a ghost. “You were there, Kael. At the First Siege of Eryndor. You saw the rift open. You saw *him* step through.”

The vision lurched. Not Valdris this time, but a battlefield under a violet sky. A tear in reality, weeping shadow. And a figure emerging—not monstrous, but sadly familiar. The memory was Marcus’s, now bleeding into Kael’s mind. The friend who had stepped into the rift to close it… and had come back changed. Empty.

“He didn’t die,” Marcus whispered, his gaze locked on the Obsidian Heart. “He was *unmade*. Woven into the fabric of this place. This… thing.” He gestured to the artifact with a hand that trembled with rage, not weakness. “It doesn’t just manipulate time, warrior. It consumes souls to fuel its rewriting. And I have spent my life feeding it, pruning the timeline, searching for the one key that could undo that day and pull him back from the weave.”

Lira, who had been frantically scanning her elven glyphs etched on the walls, froze. “The temporal collapses… the rifts you’ve engineered across Valdris… they weren’t conquest. They were experiments. You’ve been trying to reverse-engineer the Heart’s own function.”
