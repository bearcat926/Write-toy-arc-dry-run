# Chapter 68: The Shattered Moment

The shard’s command was absolute. *RECLAIM.* It was not a suggestion in Kael’s mind, but a fundamental law, like gravity. His body moved, Dawnbreaker’s light a pale echo against the deeper, silent glow of the Sanctum’s heart-stone. He crossed the polished floor, the knowledge mapping the flow of energy in the air, the precise pressure points in the pedestal of the central dais.

“Kael, wait!” Lira’s voice was sharp, cutting through the thrum in his veins. She stood at the entrance, her elven eyes wide with more than scholarly awe. “The inscriptions… they speak of a sacrifice. A lock requires a key, but sometimes the key is… consumed.”

The shard pulsed, dismissing her words as static. *Irrelevant. The vessel is prepared. You are the hand. Reclaim what was lost.*

He reached the dais. At its center lay a depression, a perfect circle of obsidian so dark it seemed to drink the light. And within it, resting as if placed there moments ago, was a fragment—a splinter of the same pulsating, night-black substance as the shard in his pocket, but larger, sharper. It hummed with a frequency that made his teeth ache.

The knowledge sang. *The Heart’s left ventricle. Damaged in the first Sundering. You will mend it.*

His hand, sheathed in armored gauntlet, moved of its own volition toward the splinter. The air grew thick, charged. The luminescent moss on the distant walls dimmed as if in deference.

“Don’t touch it, you fool!” The voice was not Lira’s. It was cold, commanding, and utterly familiar.

Kael’s head snapped sideways, the shard’s control flickering in surprise. There, on the far side of the Sanctum, stepping from the shadows of a carved archway as if they were a cloak he’d worn all along, stood Marcus. His dark armor drank the faint light, and in his hand, he held not a sword, but a slender, metallic rod that hummed with a counter-frequency to the Heart’s pulse. Behind him, two Shadow Council agents melted into view.

“The Sunstrider clan,” Marcus said, his voice echoing in the vast chamber. “Always reaching for the light, never understanding the cost of the burn. The Heart is not for you to ‘reclaim.’ It is to be controlled.”

The shard in Kael’s pocket seared with pain and fury. *THREAT. REMOVE. RECLAIM.*
