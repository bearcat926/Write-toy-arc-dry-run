# Chapter 97: The Bleeding Hour

The accusation hung, unresolved, a crack in the foundation of their quest. Kael didn't move, the weight of his newfound heritage pinning him as much as the unseen eyes of his ancestors.

“Not knew,” Lira corrected, her voice a frail whisper against the Chamber’s vastness. Her fingers brushed the burnt edge of the parchment. “Suspected. In fragments. Legends of the Oath-Clan were whispers, Kael, scattered among the forbidden texts of Eryndor’s deepest vaults. A clan that swore to a sun god while bleeding shadow. A contradiction meant to be forgotten.”

“Yet you brought me here,” Kael said, his voice regaining a sliver of its old edge, now honed on betrayal. “To *this* place. To the Obsidian Heart. You didn’t suspect *that*?”

“I brought you because the Heart called for you,” she insisted, her scholar’s passion igniting. “Because all prophecy threads pointed to one who walked between light and dark.
