# Chapter 58: The Echo of Ruin

Silence was a weight. It pressed on Kael, deeper than the tons of stone and ice entombing him. He lay in a pocket of darkness, a cathedral of ruin carved by the glacier’s final collapse. Dust, fine as powdered bone, moted in the single, weak beam of light filtering from a crack high above. He was alive, but the thought brought no relief. He could feel the Obsidian Heart not in his hands, but in his blood. A slow, black pulse that synchronized with his own heartbeat, a foreign rhythm that whispered of cracks in time.

*Lira.* The name was a shard of clarity in the disorientation. He had to find her. Pushing himself up, agony lanced through his shoulder and a dozen other screaming points of protest. Dawnbreaker lay beside him, its opalescent blade dark and silent. The Sun-glow was gone. He reached for it, not with his hand, but with the part of him that had always felt its warmth. There was only the cold echo from the Heart.

Above, in the fractured remnants of the glacier’s crown, Lira moved like a ghost. The great cistern was now a tomb, a open wound in the mountain’s side. She picked her way across jumbled ice and shattered rock, her elven senses stretched thin, searching for a trace of him. The silver thread of the glacier’s life-song was utterly severed. In its place, a dissonant hum made her teeth ache—the lingering resonance of the Obsidian Heart’s activation.

Then, she heard it. A scraping, rhythmic and faint, from beneath a landslide of blue ice. She scrambled towards it, her scholar’s robes torn, her hands raw. “Kael!”

The answer was not a voice, but a vibration through the stone. Lira placed her palm flat on a massive ice slab, and felt the distinct, deliberate pattern of a warrior’s coded strike—three slow, two fast. He was alive. Alive, but trapped.
