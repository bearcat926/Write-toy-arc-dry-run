# Chapter 18: The Compass of Scars

The silence between them on the *Silver Fin’s* deck was a living thing, fed by the creak of timber and the whisper of the unnatural calm that had fallen over the sea. Kael leaned against the rail, staring at the water. It was dark, not with depth, but with a strange, suspended quality, as if the ocean itself were holding its breath. Lira’s copied map was clutched in his fist, the parchment damp. It showed their course as a faint, eager line toward the frost-rimmed coast of Nordmark.

A soft footstep. Lira came to stand beside him, her elven eyes missing nothing. “You’ve been gripping that map so hard, you’ll smudge the mountains.”

Kael forced his hand to relax. “The mountains aren’t what concern me.” He gestured at the dead air. “No wind. The sails hang like shrouds. This is not natural.”
