# Chapter 41: The Vein of Hours

The world dissolved into shards of screaming silver. One moment, Kael was staring at the frost on his own skin, the next, the glacier was gone. He stood on a plain of smooth, black glass under a sky choked with constellations he’d never seen. The air tasted of ozone and old sorrow. Before him, suspended in nothingness, pulsed a heart. It was fist-sized, made of what looked like cooled volcanic rock, shot through with veins of dim, pulsating amethyst light. The Obsidian Heart.

It didn't beat. It *remembered*. Images flooded Kael—not memories, but possibilities, timelines branching and withering like poisoned roots. He saw Valdris consumed by a tide of silent, grey shadows. He saw Eryndor’s forests turned to crystal. He saw Lira, old and alone, her eyes empty. He saw his own hands, not of flesh, but of swirling temporal frost, reaching for the Heart and grasping only smoke.

A voice, not a sound but a pressure in his mind, spoke. *TO CHANGE THE END IS TO UNMAKE THE BEGINNING. EVERY STEP BACKWARDS DEMANDS A FOOTPRINT ERASED.*

The pain was not of body, but of existence. He felt the weight of un-lived moments, the agony of choices unmade. He saw a flicker of a life where he never held Dawnbreaker, where Lira never sought the Sunstriders, where the Shadow Council had already won. It was a future so bleak it felt more real than his present.

Kael screamed into the void, but the sound was swallowed by the glass plain. He fought for an anchor, for something true. He found Lira’s face in his mind—her determined brow, the smudge of ink on her cheek from the star-chart. He held onto it, a single, brilliant thread in the spinning chaos.

The vision shattered.

He collapsed back into his body on the frost-rimed ground, gasping. The physical cold was a shocking comfort compared to the abyssal emptiness he’d just witnessed. Lira was kneeling over him, her hands on his shoulders, her face pale with terror.

“Kael! Your eyes—they were white, then purple, then… I couldn’t see them at all!”
