# Chapter 59: The Fracture Between

Kael’s world returned in shards. Pain was the first clear note—a deep, grinding ache in his shoulder, a multitude of smaller stabs from ice-shrapnel. The second was the sound: a slow, rhythmic dripping in the profound dark. Water, not blood. A small mercy.

He pushed himself up, grit crunching beneath his palms. A single shaft of moonlight lanced through a crack in the collapsed ceiling, illuminating a narrow cavern of debris. Dawnbreaker lay a few feet away, its inner fire banked to a sullen emerald glow. He reached for it, and the sword seemed to sigh, a pulse of warmth flowing up his arm. It did not heal the pain, but it acknowledged it.

“Lira?” His voice was a raw croak. The silence answered with the endless drip.

Panic, cold and sharp, pierced the haze of injury. He scrambled, shifting slabs of frozen stone that were lighter than they should be, the glacier’s structure utterly compromised. Then he heard it—a faint, chanted string of words in Old Elvish, coming from beyond a wall of fractured blue ice.

He swung Dawnbreaker. The blade bit into the ancient ice not with a clang, but with a hiss, and the wall came apart in a cascade of crystals. Beyond, in a similar pocket of survival, Lira knelt. She was unmarked, her hands pressed flat to the floor. Around her, the remaining veins of obsidian pulsed with a faint, hungry light.

“It is not gone,” she whispered, not looking at him. Her eyes were closed, her brow furrowed in concentration. “The Heart… it shattered the glacier to free itself. But it left… echoes. Hooks in the rock. In time itself.”

“What did we do, Lira?” Kael asked, the question heavy as a tombstone. The memory of his hand on that sphere, of the cold that sought to erase him, was a fresh wound in his mind.

“We answered its first demand,” she said, finally opening her eyes. They were shadowed with a weariness older than the mountain. “We sacrificed a place. It awoke. Now it will seek a person.”
