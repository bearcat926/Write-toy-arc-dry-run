# Chapter 62: The Heart's Demand

The knowledge was a poison sweet as honey. Kael didn’t have to see the path; the shard etched it onto his soul. Left. Three steps. The frost-rimed stone would bear his weight. A low, groaning fissure lay beyond, but it was stable. For now.

He obeyed. Each movement sent jagged lightning through his shoulder, but the shard’s whispers were a silken counterpoint, numbing the edges, promising *focus*. Dawnbreaker, slung across his back, felt inert, a dead weight compared to the thrumming life in his fist.

“*She lies ahead. The scholar. The sun touches her skin, but not her heart. She is afraid of you. Not of the darkness… of the light you refuse.*”

Lira. The thought of her was a splash of cold clarity. Kael’s steps faltered. “Why do you show me this?” he growled into the gloom, his breath fogging.

The whisper became a laugh, dry as tomb-leaves. “*I show you what is. What was. What could be. She seeks a key to undo the knot. I am the shears. You are the hand.*”

A faint, golden radiance began to bleed into the tunnel ahead, the promise of an exit. But with it came a new knowing, sharp and brutal. A vision that wasn’t a vision: Lira, her face etched with horror, not looking at him, but *through* him, at something vast and dark he could not see. Her mouth formed a word he couldn’t hear. Then the image fractured, and he saw himself standing before a dais of black glass—the true Obsidian Heart. Dawnbreaker was raised, not in a strike of defiance, but in offering. The scene reeked of sacrifice.

“*Cost is memory. Cost is self. You will pay it. You have always been paying it.*”

“No,” Kael rasped, squeezing his eyes shut. The pain in his shoulder was a distant, throbbing anchor to reality. He was Kael of the Sunstriders. His duty was clear. Protect. Defend. Destroy this thing.
