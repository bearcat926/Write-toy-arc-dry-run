# Chapter 1: Ashes of the Sunstrider

The funeral pyre painted the canyon walls in amber and shadow, and Kael watched his father burn.

Thirty-two Sunstriders remained. He had counted them twice, standing among the cracked obsidian pillars that had given their stronghold its name. Thirty-two out of three hundred. Marcus and the Shadow Council had come in the dead of the moon, riding black-sailed longships up from the Thalassian coast. They had not come for plunder. They had come for the map.

Kael's hand tightened around Dawnbreaker's leather-wrapped grip. The ancient sword hummed faintly against his palm, as if mourning too. Its pale radiance had dimmed to a guttering whisper — a reflection, perhaps, of its wielder's spirit.

"You shouldn't hold it so openly," said a voice behind him.

He turned. The woman stood at the canyon's edge, half-hidden in the violet dusk. Elven — unmistakably so, from the angular symmetry of her features to the silver-threaded dark hair that fell past her shoulders. She wore the robes of the Eryndoran Academy, travel-stained and torn at the hem. A scholar's chain hung around her throat, heavy with seals Kael did not recognize.

"Who are you?" he said.

"My name is Lira Vaethis. I've been sent by the Academy's High Archivists." She stepped forward, her pale eyes fixed on Dawnbreaker. "And if you continue carrying that blade in the open, you will lead the Shadow Council straight to what remains of your people."

"The Council already knows where we are."

"No. They know where your *father* was." Her gaze softened, though her voice did not. "They believed he was the last one who could read the Obsidian Rites. Now they will look elsewhere — unless you give them reason not to."

The pyre crackled. Kael's father had died rather than speak. He had bought his clan those hours with silence and steel, and Kael had carried him the final mile with Dawnbreaker still slick in his grip.

"What do you want from me?" he asked.

Lira produced a folded parchment from within her robes, its edges blackened as though rescued from a fire. She held it out between two careful fingers.

"The Obsidian Heart is not a legend," she said. "It was hidden centuries ago — split into fragments and scattered across all four kingdoms. The Shadow Council has been searching for them. They found the first in Thalassia. The second is in Nordmark, and my sources say Marcus rides for it within the week."

Kael stared at the parchment. His father's handwriting. His father's seal.

"This is a map," he whispered.

"It is *the* map. Your father entrusted it to the Academy years ago, in case something like this happened." Lira's expression hardened with purpose. "The Heart can rewind time itself, Kael. In the wrong hands, it could unmake everything — every kingdom, every age, every life. But in the right hands—"

"In the right hands, what?"

She hesitated. It was the first crack in her composure.

"History can be corrected," she said quietly.
