# Chapter 4: The Cartographer's Ghost

The map was wrong.

Lira had spread it across the charred remains of Thornwall's only standing table, weighing the corners with stones and a broken lantern. By candlelight, the ink appeared to breathe — rivers shifting by a hair's breadth, mountain ranges bending where no bend should be.

"This passage," she murmured, tracing a line of Elvish script with a slender finger, "was not here three days ago. I've studied the Caeloran Atlas since I was a child. This corridor between Eryndor and Thalassia... it appeared after the Burning."

Kael leaned over her shoulder, though the script was little more than elegant scratches to his eyes. "Things don't just appear on maps."

"Ordinary maps, no." Lira pulled her hand back as though the parchment had grown hot. "But this atlas was inked by Serethiel the Endless, a cartographer who mapped not geography but *possibility*. Every line represents a thread of what could be." She paused. "Or what has been undone."

The candle guttered. Kael straightened, his hand instinctively finding Dawnbreaker's hilt. The blade had been quiet since the shadow creature's dissolution — too quiet, like a watchdog that had stopped barking for reasons you didn't want to understand.

"You think the Obsidian Heart is rewriting the world," he said. It wasn't a question.

"I think it already has." Lira's voice carried the careful steadiness of someone who had arrived at a terrifying conclusion hours ago and had been assembling the courage to speak it aloud. "The villages burned before you reached them, Kael. Not by days. What if Thornwall didn't fall to Marcus or the Shadow Council? What if it fell because someone already turned the Heart — and we are living in the aftermath?"

He wanted to argue. He wanted to draw Dawnbreaker and cut clean through her theory the way the blade cut through shadow. But he remembered the road to Thornwall, the eerie emptiness, the way even the crows had seemed surprised to see him, as if he'd arrived somewhere he no longer belonged.

"How far?" he asked.

Lira pointed to the impossible corridor on the map. "If this passage is real — if reality has been folded to create it — then the Heart is not buried in the ruins of Valdris as the old prophecies claim. It's here." Her finger rested on a mark that hadn't existed when she'd unrolled the parchment. A symbol, black as pitch, shaped like a bleeding star. "Beneath the Ashenmere. A day's ride south."

Kael studied the mark. Dawnbreaker pulsed once at his hip — not with warmth, but with something colder. Recognition, perhaps. Or warning.

"Then we ride at dawn," he said.
