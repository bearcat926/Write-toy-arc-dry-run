# Chapter 3: The Fracture of Trust

The shadow creature’s remains scattered like ash on the wind, leaving only the smell of ozone and old parchment in the air. Kael sat heavily on a fallen log, the glow of Dawnbreaker fading to a soft pulse against his palm as the immediate threat vanished.

Lira knelt beside him, her slender fingers already unpacking a kit of salves and clean linen. “The cut is shallow, but tainted. It will fester if not seen to.”

Kael grunted, offering his forearm. “You knew what it was. Before.”

“An Echomorrow.” She dabbed a cool, herbal paste over the gash. Her touch was precise, clinical. “A parasitic temporal fragment. Not truly alive. Drawn to powerful chronal disturbances… or those connected to them.” Her silver eyes met his, holding a question she didn’t voice.

He looked away, toward the scar on the hill where Thornwall had been. “The Heart. That’s what you think it is.”

“It’s what I know it is.” Lira’s voice was firm, yet quiet. “The Obsidian Heart is not just a legend in Eryndor’s archives. It’s a recorded calamity. A nexus point where time… frays.” She finished tying the bandage. “The Echomorrow was likely pulled here by the residual trauma of the village, a wound in the timeline. Or…”

“Or by me.” Kael finished, the words heavy. Dawnbreaker hummed faintly on his back, a sympathetic, mournful sound. He remembered the moment in the flames, the desperate wish to have been there sooner, to turn back the dawn.
