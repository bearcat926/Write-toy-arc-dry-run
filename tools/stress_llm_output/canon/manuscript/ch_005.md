# Chapter 5: The Weight of Ash

Kael stood watch until dawn, but it was not the dark he guarded against. It was the silence where Thornwall’s voices should have been. When the sun’s first light bled grey across the horizon, Lira emerged from the ruined tavern, the changing map held tight in her hands.

“It’s stabilized,” she said, her voice hollow with lack of sleep. She laid it on the same charred table. The rivers and mountains had ceased their dance. A single route now glowed faintly, a line of gold cutting south through the heart of Valdris. It ended at a single, ominous point marked with a symbol Kael recognized: the double-eclipse sigil of the Shadow Council.

“Their stronghold,” he breathed, his hand instinctively going to Dawnbreaker’s hilt. “It’s leading us to Marcus.”

“It’s not leading us,” Lira corrected, her finger tracing the path. “It’s *revealing*. The map isn’t just a tool. It’s a recording. It remembers the path the Obsidian Heart took last… or perhaps the path it *will* take. The fracture in time I sensed isn’t random. It’s a scar, left by its passage.” She looked up, her elven eyes wide with dawning comprehension. “It’s not just a weapon, Kael. It’s an anchor. And someone has been dragging it through our history, tearing the fabric of things as they go.”

The weight of her words settled on Kael like a physical burden. He thought of Thornwall, not just burning, but *unmade*. “You’re saying the Heart was here?”

“Or will be. Or was,” she said, frustrated by the language’s limitations
