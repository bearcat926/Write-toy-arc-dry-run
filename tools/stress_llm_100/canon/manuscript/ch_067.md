# Chapter 67: The Sculptor and the Clay

The shard’s knowledge pulsed with a new urgency, a silent scream in his blood. *There. The Sanctum. Go.*

Before Kael stood the true heart of the buried city. It was not a building, but a cavern shaped by hands, its walls carved with spiraling patterns that seemed to shift when not looked at directly. At its center, on a dais of smooth black stone, rested the Obsidian Heart. It was no larger than a human fist, a lustrous, faceted sphere of darkness that did not reflect the phosphorescent moss light, but consumed it. The air around it shimmered, thick with a pressure that made Kael’s ears pop and his vision swim with after-images of moments not yet lived.

He stepped forward, drawn by the shard’s compulsion. The map on his soul showed a safe path, a dance of specific steps to avoid the temporal distortions that swirled like invisible whirlpools in the air. He took one step, then another.

But the Heart was not passive. A wave of sensation washed over him—not memory, but *potential*. He saw Lira, her face contorted in grief, kneeling in ashes that were not Valdrisian. He saw Marcus, his shadow form solidifying into something triumphant and monstrous, his laughter echoing in a sunless sky. He saw himself, older, scarred, Dawnbreaker shattered in his hand, standing alone atop a mountain of his clansmen’s bones. *This is the price,* the shard whispered, its tone awed. *To see all paths is to walk all of them at once.*

“Kael! The patterns on the walls… they’re a warning!” Lira’s voice was thin, strained. She hadn’t moved from the cavern entrance, her staff planted firmly. Elven lore, not a magical shard, held her back. “It’s a labyrinth of time. Step inside its influence, and it will unwind you.”

The shard’s command was absolute: *Take it. It is yours. You are strong enough to bear the seeing.*

He looked at his hand, at the scar where the first shard had bonded to his flesh. He was its vessel, its perfect clay. The realization was a cold sliver of clarity in the heat of its demand. It had guided him, preserved him, but for what? To deliver him here, to this nexus of terrible power, for its own inscrutable purpose.

He was a warrior of the Sunstriders. He broke things that threatened his people. He did not serve.
