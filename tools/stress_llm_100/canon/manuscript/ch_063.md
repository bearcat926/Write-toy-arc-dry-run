# Chapter 63: The Price of Knowing

The shard’s knowledge was a relentless tide. It told Kael not only where to step, but *how* to breathe to conserve energy, *when* to pause as the ice above settled with a sigh like a sleeping beast. It was a map written on his nerves. He moved with a jerky, certain grace, ignoring the fire in his shoulder, the grit in his eyes. Lira followed close, her own exhaustion a palpable silence behind him.

“The light ahead,” she whispered, her voice raw. “Not natural. See the green tint?”

Kael didn’t need to see. The shard *knew*. “Bioluminescent moss. A fissure to the surface. The air is warmer there. We’ll be exposed.”

He crawled forward, the shard hot in his palm. The knowledge shifted, became a cold current. *Danger. Not the ice. Not the exposure. Her.*

He stopped, turning in the cramped tunnel. In the faint, sickly green glow from the moss ahead, Lira’s face was gaunt, her scholar’s eyes wide and fixed on his hand. “Kael… your eyes. When you listen to it, they go black. Like polished night.”

“It’s showing us the way,” he said, and the words felt thick, not entirely his own.

“It’s showing you *its* way,” she countered, fear sharpening her tone. “The cost, Kael. It’s not just a trinket. It’s a piece of the Heart. It’s rewriting you.”

The shard pulsed, a wave of contempt for her doubt. *She sees fragments. You see the whole. The glacier was a test. You passed. The Heart calls its children home.*

*Where?* Kael thought, the question direct, insistent.

The answer was not a location, but a feeling: a gravitational pull toward the southeast, toward the black volcanic ranges on the border of Valdris and Eryndor. The shard hummed with a terrible, triumphant certainty. *The Obsidian Spire. Where time bleeds. Where we will be made whole.*

“Kael, we have to go north,” Lira urged, touching his arm. The contact felt distant, like a raindrop on steel. “The rendezvous at Ironhold—”

“North is a lie,” Kael heard himself say, the voice calm, alien. “The Sunstriders, the Shadow Council, they’re chasing echoes. The Heart is not a weapon to be wielded. It is a key to be turned. And it is calling me.”

He saw the understanding dawn in her eyes, followed by a profound grief. She saw it clearly now: the warrior she’d traveled with was becoming a vessel.
