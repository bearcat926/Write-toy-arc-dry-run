# Chapter 77: The Unraveling Knot

The lattice was gone. In its place, a pulsating obsidian core floated where the Heart had been, its surface now smooth as a black pearl. The temporal storm had not ceased—it had been swallowed, absorbed into this new, refined terror. The silence was absolute, a pressure against Kael’s eardrums.

Lira lay crumpled against a wall, her breath a shallow rasp. The elven script on her skin was faded, almost gone. “It… it didn’t destroy the moment,” she whispered, her voice thin as parchment. “It ingested it. That last moment of defiance… it became fuel.”

Kael stood before the pearl, Dawnbreaker hanging limp in his grip. The sword’s light was a bare, guttering candle. He felt the pull of the Heart’s new form not on his body, but on his timeline.
