# Chapter 3: The Weight of Glass

The amber light from the Obsidian Heart’s chamber did not chase away the shadows in Kael’s mind; it only made them sharper. He leaned against the cool stone wall outside, the whispers now a dull, persistent ache behind his eyes. In his palm, the vial of liquid amber Lira had extracted glowed faintly, a tiny sun of captured possibility.

Lira emerged, her elven features pale, her satchel slung tightly. She didn't look at the vial. "The inscriptions speak of the Amber Vision," she said, her voice low. "It shows a pivotal moment, a thread of fate the Heart could unravel or strengthen. But to view it is to accept a debt. Time is not a river to be diverted without consequence, Kael. It is a tapestry. Pull one thread, and another elsewhere frays to nothing."

"I have to know," Kael said, his voice rough. The memory of his clan's eastern outpost—fire, screams, the Shadow Council's banners—was a wound that never closed. Had there been a moment to stop it? A single, missable second?

Before Lira could protest further, he unstoppered the vial. The amber poured into his open palm, not like liquid, but like light, soaking into his skin. The world dissolved.

*He saw not fire, but a frost-touched forest at dawn. His brother, Ryn, stood alone, watching the horizon. Kael felt a phantom urge to call out, to warn him. Then he saw it—a flicker of movement in the trees, a glint of steel. Ryn turned, too slowly. In the vision, Kael’s own hand, gripping Dawnbreaker, came into frame. He was there. He was supposed to be there. But he wasn’t. The vision shattered.*

Kael gasped, stumbling back against the wall. The amber in his hand was gone, but a new coldness had settled in his bones. It wasn’t just a memory. It was a possibility, a place where his choice had already been made.

"The cost," Lira whispered, her eyes wide with understanding. "You saw the thread. To use the Heart now, to try to grasp that moment… it will cost you the *memory* of your brother's face. The Heart doesn't change the past; it consumes your connection to it."

The weight of Dawnbreaker on his back felt immense. The quest had been for justice, for vengeance. Now it felt like a trap, a choice between a future defined by loss, and a past he would have to surrender piece by piece to change.

A cold laugh echoed from the temple entrance. Kael and Lira spun, hands on their weapons.
