# Chapter 88: The Unraveling Past

The vision of Valdris’s fall did not fade. It seared itself behind Kael’s eyes, not as a memory, but as a wound. He saw the city walls not crumbling from siege engines, but *dissolving*, the stone turning to shimmering sand that flowed upward into a bruised sky. The Sunstrider banners that flew there were not torn by wind, but unraveled, thread by golden thread, until only the poles remained, skeletal fingers pointing at nothing.

Lira’s voice was a distant, urgent whisper. “Kael, it’s not showing the past. It’s showing a *potential* past. A timeline that was consumed.”

He forced his gaze from the flickering maelstrom within the fractured obsidian. The chamber felt colder, the air tasting of static and ozone. “Consumed?”

“The Heart doesn’t just manipulate time,” Lira explained, her scholar’s mind racing even as her face was pale. “It *digests* it. It needed energy to function, to heal itself after you struck it. It’s… harvesting. Pulling moments of catastrophe from the temporal stream and drinking the chaos.”

The implications slammed into Kael. They hadn’t silenced the artifact. They had made it desperate, and in its desperation, it was scouring the history of the world for moments of great suffering to feast upon. The Fall of Valdris was merely the first course.

His grip on Dawnbreaker tightened, the sword’s faint, steadying glow a stark contrast to the Heart’s sickly pulse. For the first time since taking up the blade, its light felt insufficient. He was a warrior. He understood axes and enemies. This was a hunger that could unravel the very reason a battle was fought.

A boot scraped on rubble behind them.

Kael spun, Dawnbreaker coming up in a defensive arc. From the deeper shadows of the collapsed archway, a figure emerged, not with the rush of attack, but with a deliberate, measured pace. Marcus, commander of the Shadow Council, his dark armor scuffed and torn. His face, usually a mask of cold command, was etched with something close to awe, and a deeper, more personal horror as he looked at the pulsing Heart.
