# Chapter 80: The Warrior's Choice

The golden light of Dawnbreaker flickered in Kael's hand, not from weakness, but from the impossible physics of the Sanctum. He watched, his warrior's heart chilled, as a decade of erosion bloomed across a stone cherub's face in the space between breaths. It wasn't decay; it was *history*, being forcibly written and then erased.

"He's not controlling it," Lira whispered, her voice tight with dawning horror. She had pressed herself against a relatively stable archway, her eyes darting, cataloging the chaos. "The Heart is consuming him to fuel the paradox. He's the furnace, not the blacksmith."

Marcus, or what was left of him, tilted his head. The movement was jerky, puppet-like. The obsidian growth pulsed, and a wave of temporal distortion radiated outward. Kael braced, but it didn't strike him. It struck *behind* him. He heard Lira's sharp gasp and spun.

Where she had stood, a younger Lira now blinked in confusion, her robes simpler, her eyes wide with a scholar's curiosity, not a survivor's fear. She looked at Kael, then at the terrifying figure of Marcus, and opened her mouth to form a question from a year ago.

"No!" Kael lunged toward her, but the floor between them turned to quicksand of moments, sucking at his boots. For every step forward, he felt memories tear loose—the taste of his mother's bread, the smell of the training yards after rain, the color of his first dawn as a Sunstrider. They were being skimmed from him, the cost of traversing this fractured ground.

He was paying in his past to reach her.

Dawnbreaker pulsed, sensing its wielder's desperation. Its light was no longer just a weapon; it was a tether to *now*. Kael made his choice. He would not let Lira be lost to a past iteration. He poured not just strength, but identity into the blade. *I am Kael. I am here. I remember.*

The golden radiance solidified around him, a second skin. It burned away the temporal erosion, but the pain was excruciating. He was choosing to experience the full weight of every stolen moment at once. He staggered the last few steps and grabbed the arm of the younger Lira. "You're not real. Not anymore."

Her eyes, so full of untainted wonder, focused on him. "I feel real."

"You are a memory he is using to break me," Kael said, his voice raw. He raised Dawnbreaker.
