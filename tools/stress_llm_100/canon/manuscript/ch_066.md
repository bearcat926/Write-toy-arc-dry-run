# Chapter 66: The Echoing Ruin

The knowledge guided Kael’s feet onto the moss-softened stones of the sunken city. It did not guide his heart, which clenched with a strange, hollow ache at the sight of the elegant, collapsed arches and silent, watchful statues draped in luminescent green. This place was old beyond reckoning, a corpse of a civilization that had perhaps once spoken to the stars. The shard pulsed, pointing him toward a central ziggurat, its steps eroded but intact.

“Kael, wait,” Lira breathed, her voice echoing slightly in the vastness. She knelt by a fallen pillar, tracing carvings with a reverent finger. Her scholarly awe was a tangible thing, a warmth Kael remembered fondly, like sunlight on a distant shore. “This isn’t Eryndor or Valdris script. It’s older. Pre-Collapse.”

He knew. The shard had told him the moment they entered. It was Thalassian, but not the elegant, flowing script of the sea-elves. This was archaic, angular, and familiar in a way that made his skin crawl. The knowledge whispered its translation without him asking.

*‘We bound the flow to cheat the tomb. The Heart remembers, and the debt comes due.’*

“It’s a warning,” Kael said, his voice flat. He didn’t look at her. His gaze was fixed on the ziggurat’s summit, where a darker pool of shadow seemed to coalesce.

“A warning about what? About the Obsidian Heart?” Lira scrambled to her feet, her excitement warring with concern. “Kael, what did you see? You’re trembling.”
