# Chapter 28: The Scholar's Gambit

The light in Kael’s eyes was guttering, a candle in a gale. Lira’s healing eldritch glow, a tapestry of silver and green, was not enough. It soothed, but did not replenish. She could feel the void where his seconds had been—a silent, screaming hollow in his spirit that her magic could not fill. The chamber’s cold, once a distant concern, was now a physical predator, seeping into his bones.

His words were a rasp, each one costing him. “Leave… the Heart. Take Dawnbreaker. Warn Valdris.”

“No.” The word was steel in her own ears, surprising her. Lira the scholar, who chronicled history, not made it. But here, in this glacial tomb with death scenting the air, a different kind of knowledge asserted itself. She looked from Kael’s failing form to the Obsidian Heart, hovering in its column of frozen light. It pulsed with a slow, abyssal rhythm, like a black star.

The cost was clear. The scars on time. The bleeding of self. But the alternative, she realized, watching Kael’s breath cloud and thin, was absolute.

*You study to understand,* a voice in her memory chided. Her old mentor in the Eryndor archives. *You do not understand to wield.*

But what was understanding, if not to be used? What was all her lore about the temporal arts, about the Heart’s rumored sentience, if not for this moment?

She approached it, her scholar’s mind racing, cataloging every warning from every crumbling text. The Heart did not manipulate time like a river; it frayed the tapestry, pulling threads. The user paid in threads of their own life, their own history. It was a parasite of possibility.

Kael gave a choked groan behind her, the sound spurring her on. She reached out, not with the reckless greed of a conqueror, but with the precise, seeking touch of a student. Her fingers brushed the crystalline surface.

Cold beyond description. Not of ice, but of nullity. And then—a whisper. Not of sound, but of *potential*. It showed her a thousand branching paths. In one, she turned away, and Kael died on the ice, his light extinguished. In another, she fled with Dawnbreaker, and Marcus’s forces swarmed the chamber a moment too late. In a third, a shimmering, fragile thread, Kael rose, healed.
