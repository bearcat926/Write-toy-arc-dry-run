# Chapter 84: The Price of a Moment

The silence broke with the sound of Kael’s breathing—ragged, harsh. He was on one knee, Dawnbreaker’s point digging into the age-fused stone to hold him up. The golden light was dimmer, pulsing in time with the frantic beat of his own heart. Across the shattered Sanctum, Lira knelt, her hands pressed flat against the floor.

“The veins,” she whispered, her voice thin with awe. “They’re fading. The temporal bleed… it’s healing.”

Kael pushed himself upright. The oppressive weight of eternity had lifted, leaving only a dull ache in his bones. He looked at his sword. Where Marcus had stood, there was a perfect, human-shaped absence in the dust—a silhouette burned into the floor by a light that was not meant for this world.

“What did we do?” Kael’s voice was rough.

Lira stood, brushing millennia of grime from her robes. Her eyes, wide and luminous in the gloom, fixed on Dawnbreaker. “You did not strike the man, Kael. You struck the connection. The Heart’s power needs a conduit, a living anchor to focus its chaos. For a moment, you severed it.”

“A moment.” He followed her gaze to the scorched outline. “And Marcus?”

The scholar gestured to the shadows pooling unnaturally in a corner, deeper than before. They seemed to swirl, not with dust, but with silent, echoing whispers. “The energy had to go somewhere. It didn’t destroy him. It… *unmoored* him.”

As if summoned by her words, a voice slithered from the gathering darkness—a voice Kael knew, yet twisted. It held Marcus’s cadence but layered with a thousand overlapping whispers, each from a different time.

*“You… showed me… the cost…”* The shadow-form coalesced, not into a man, but into a shifting pillar of obsidian and mist. Within it, flashes of different eras flickered: a boy with Marcus’s eyes weeping in a burning city, an old man dictating to scribes, a corpse in a field. *“The Heart does not manipulate time. It trades it. A moment of true power for a lifetime of erosion… for the wielder, or the world.”*

Kael raised Dawnbreaker, its light a feeble challenge against the swirling chronal wound.

*“You froze my moment, Sunstrider,”* the echo-Marcus hissed, the whispers rising to a chorus. *“Now I have all of them.”* The shadow began to spread, not consuming, but *replacing*. The stone beneath it aged, cracked, and withered to sand in seconds. *“I will choose the perfect one.”*
