# Chapter 20: The Echo in the Ice

The cave behind the waterfall was not a cave. The glacial heartbeat had been a lure, and now, standing before it, Kael saw the truth. What appeared to be a wall of ancient, blue ice was instead a perfectly smooth archway, carved with geometric precision. No frost clung to its surface. It was cold, but a dry, sleeping cold, not the wet, biting hunger of the fjord.

“Sunforged runes,” Lira breathed, her fingers hovering over the carvings without touching. “But inverted. They’re meant to contain, not illuminate.”

Kael’s hand tightened on Dawnbreaker’s hilt. The sword hummed, a low vibration that traveled up his arm and settled in his teeth. It felt like a warning, but also a recognition. This place was anathema to its light, yet intrinsically aware of it.

“Do we go through?” he asked, his voice swallowed by the immense, quiet pressure of the ice.

“Is that even a question?” Lira’s gaze was fixed on the runes. “The Heart is here. I can… sense its absence. Like a silent scream in the fabric of things.” She finally looked at him, her elven eyes reflecting the eerie glow of the ice. “It’s not manipulating time, Kael. It’s *severed* from it. That’s the source of the stillness. We’re standing at a wound.”

He took the first step. The threshold offered no resistance, only a profound shift. The air inside was perfectly still, and the light was a diffuse, sourceless grey. They stood in a vast, circular chamber, the walls of living ice enclosing a space that felt more like a cathedral than a tomb. In the center, resting on a basalt plinth that seemed to defy the glacial floor, was a object.
