# Chapter 56: The Forgetting

The world cracked.

Not with a sound, but with a tremor that began in the dais beneath Kael’s feet and radiated outward, a shudder through the very bones of the mountain. The frozen vault, which had held its breath for centuries, exhaled. The veins of living obsidian flickered, their inner light guttering as if in alarm.

“It knows,” Lira breathed, her voice tight. Her scholar’s mind was racing, cataloging the vibrations, the subsonic hum now rising into an audible, grinding groan. “The glacier is a vessel. Taking the Heart will collapse it.”

Kael didn’t look away from the sphere. It was no longer just a void. In its perfect blackness, he saw—or felt—the echoes of moments not yet lived. A flash of Lira’s smile, wide and unguarded. The weight of Dawnbreaker in his hand, not against frost, but against steel, under a sky choked with smoke. A child’s laughter, echoing in a hall he didn’t recognize.

*Love,* the Heart whispered, not in words, but in the hollow ache that bloomed behind his ribs. *Take me. Protect them. Rule them.*

“Kael, we must go.” Lira’s hand was on his arm, her grip firm. The floor tilted. A jagged crack split the ice-dais between his boots. “If this place comes down, it will bury the truth forever.”

*And take you with it,* his mind supplied. He looked at her then, truly looked. Her silver hair was dusted with frost, her luminous eyes wide with a scholar’s awe and a companion’s fear. He remembered her in the Eryndor archives, a smudge of ink on her cheek, explaining the lineage of dead kings. He remembered her grim smile after the ambush in Nordmark, setting his dislocated shoulder with practiced efficiency.

The memory of her was a sunlit thread in the tapestry of his life.

*What is a thread against a kingdom? Against a world?* The Heart pulsed. It offered him a future where the Shadow Council burned, where Valdris stood unbroken. All it required was a single, clean tear in the fabric of his past.

A massive pillar of ice groaned and tilted, crashing into the far wall in a cascade of shrapnel. The groan became a roar.

“The cost,” Kael said, his voice rough, swallowed by the chaos. He finally tore his gaze from the Heart to meet Lira’s. “It would make me forget you.”
