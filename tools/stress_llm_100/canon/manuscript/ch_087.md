# Chapter 87: Echo of the Makers

The vision shattered not with a crash, but with a silent implosion. Lira’s breath returned in a gasp, her hands shaking as they pressed against the cold, cracked floor. The echo of the whole, perfect Obsidian Heart—a thing of terrifying, harmonious geometry—still burned behind her eyes. It was not what she had expected. No malice, no thirst for control.

“Lira.” Kael’s voice was rough, his hand a steadying weight on her shoulder. “Your eyes—they were black. For a moment.”

“The vision… it wasn’t just a memory.” She pushed herself up, her scholar’s mind racing to categorize the impossible. “It was a blueprint. A… a key to its nature.” She looked from the fractured, pulsing Heart to Kael’s exhausted face. “The ancients who made it weren’t conquerors. They were dying.”

Kael frowned, tightening his grip on Dawnbreaker. “It’s a weapon. It erases things. I’ve seen it.”

“Is a surgeon’s knife a weapon? Or a tool for a desperate, last resort?” Lira’s voice grew stronger, the familiar cadence of discovery replacing the tremor of shock. “Their world was ending, Kael.
