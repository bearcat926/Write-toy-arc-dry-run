# Chapter 50: The Choice of Scars

Kael woke not to Lira’s watchful eyes, but to a voice inside his own skull. It was not the frost-whispers of the glacier, nor the distant, pleading echoes of unmade futures. This was deeper, older—a resonance that hummed in his very bones, synchronized with the dormant pulse of Dawnbreaker at his hip.

The cave was empty save for him. A single charred root smoked in the fire pit. Where was Lira?

He pushed himself up, and pain lanced through his temple, not the sharp agony of frost-bite, but a heavy, sucking dizziness. The world swam, and in the shimmering shadows cast by the dying embers, he saw it: a brief, shimmering outline of the cave wall, as it was, and as it would be in a hundred years, a crumbled ruin lapped by a black, placid sea. He blinked, and the vision vanished, leaving only a profound sense of loss.

*The cost is clarity,* the voice murmured from within. It was his own voice, and yet not. It was the voice of a man who had already made the choices Kael was yet to face.

Lira’s warnings from the previous night crashed back—the risk of his mind fracturing along the paths he’d walked and the paths he’d un-walked. Was this the beginning? Was the Obsidian Heart, wherever it was, calling to him through the fractures it had already caused?

He found her outside, standing at the glacier’s edge, her back to him. The wind tore at her cloak, but she stood firm, staring out at the blighted, time-scarred landscape below.

“You heard it too,” she said, without turning. Her voice was frayed. “The call.”

Kael stepped beside her, following her gaze to where a cluster of twisted, petrified trees existed simultaneously as saplings and dust. “It wasn’t a call. It was… a memory. One I haven’t made yet.”
