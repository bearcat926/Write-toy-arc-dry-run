# Chapter 7: The Blood Price

The dust of the collapsed tunnel settled like a shroud over Marcus’s still form. Kael couldn’t bring himself to look away. The man was dead, slain by his own hand, and yet the silence that followed was not the silence of victory. It was the silence of a question unanswered.

Lira’s voice cut through, soft but urgent. “Kael. The vial.”

He looked down. The small container of liquid amber was clutched so tightly in his fist that his knuckles were white. It was pulsing with a faint, internal light, a rhythm that now perfectly matched the slowing beat of his own heart. A drop of Marcus’s blood, shed in the struggle, had spattered onto its glass surface. The amber within flared, and the bloodstain vanished, absorbed into the liquid.

“What was that?” Kael breathed.

“Not ‘what’,” Lira whispered, her elven eyes wide with a scholar’s awe and a warrior’s fear. “When.” She pointed a slender finger. “Look.”

Where the blood had been, a scene was forming within the vial, clear as a window. They saw not their present, but the past. A younger Marcus, clad not in Shadow Council black but in the simple leather of a Thalassian outrider, stood on a sun-drenched cliffside. Beside him, laughing, was a woman with Kael’s own storm-grey eyes. His aunt, Miriel, lost to the border skirmishes five years ago.

Kael’s breath hitched. “That’s… not possible.”

“The Heart doesn’t just manipulate time,” Lira said, her voice hushed with revelation. “It preserves it. This vial… it’s not a piece of the Obsidian Heart. It’s a *recording*. A moment of time, bled and bottled.”

The vision in the vial shifted. The laughing scene dissolved, replaced by the same cliffside, now under a bruised, stormy sky. Miriel was gone. In her place stood the hunched, robed figure of a true Shadow Council elder, and Marcus was no longer laughing. He was on his knees
