# Chapter 16: The Commander's Gambit

The grapple hooks bit into the chalk cliff face with a series of violent, metallic crunches. Kael didn’t wait for the ropes to go taut. He moved, Dawnbreaker a searing streak of light as he severed the first line. Sparks showered onto the rocks below. Another hook found purchase. Then another. The black-shrouded figures scaling from the Shadow Council’s lead dromond moved with a silent, relentless efficiency that spoke of Marcus’s personal guard.

"They're not trying to board. They're trying to pin us down," Lira said, her voice tight. She was pressing the copied parchment map to her chest, her eyes scanning the cove’s narrow mouth. Three more enemy vessels were rounding the headland, their sails bearing the Council’s eclipsed sun.

Kael parried a thrown dagger, the steel vaporizing in Dawnbreaker’s light. His arms burned. They couldn’t fight an army, not here, not with the Heart’s location now a fragile secret between them. The vision—the cost—echoed in his mind. The map had wept not for its own sorrow, but for the world’s. The Obsidian Heart didn’t just manipulate time; it drank it, erasing moments from the fabric of existence, leaving holes that could unravel everything.

A clear space opened on the cliff’s edge directly above the cove’s entrance. And there, standing amidst his scaling troops as if on a reviewing platform, was Marcus. His commander’s cloak whipped in the wind, his face pale and stark, his eyes fixed not on Kael, but on Lira.

"The scholar holds the key," Marcus’s voice carried, eerily calm over the wind and waves. "Give it, and the Sunstrider’s blood debt is paid. The past can be… rewritten."

Kael felt a cold shock. It wasn’t a threat. It was a plea. A plea wrapped in the language of damnation. He understood then.
