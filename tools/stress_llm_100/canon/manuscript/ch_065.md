# Chapter 65: The Heart's Echo

The tunnel opened onto a ledge overlooking a vast, unnatural cavern. The ceiling was lost in a bruised purple gloom, but the space below was illuminated by veins of phosphorescent moss crawling over titanic, carved structures—what looked like the foundations of a buried city. At its center, suspended by chains of black iron thicker than a man, hung a heart. It was not flesh, but obsidian, veined with red light that pulsed in slow, rhythmic throbs. The Obsidian Heart.

The whisper in Kael’s blood became a roar. Every scrap of knowledge the shard had given him—the path, the air, the stability of stone—coalesced into a single, blazing imperative: *Go to the Heart. Touch it. End this.*

He took a step onto the ledge. The stone crumbled beneath his boot.

He did not fall. The shard’s knowledge had already told him his weight was misplaced, his balance a hair off. It had *let* him stumble as a lesson. The cost was a spray of grit into the abyss and a cold sweat on his neck. The lesson was clear: obey, or be destroyed by your own ignorance.

“Kael.” Lira’s voice was a ragged thread. She stood just behind him, her elven eyes wide, reflecting the Heart’s sickly pulse. She saw not just the artifact, but its effect on him—the slack tension in his body, the way his head tilted as if listening. “Don’t look at it. Look at *me*.”

He tried. Her face was a palimpsest of fear and fierce intellect, but the shard overlaid it with cold data: *Her life signs are elevated. She is a distraction. The Heart is the priority.*
