# Chapter 29: The Price of a Heartbeat

The glacial chamber held its breath as Marcus stepped from the shadows, his black armor drinking the dying light. A cruel smile played on his lips, his gaze fixed not on Kael, but on the pulsating Obsidian Heart.

"The Sunstrider's sun finally sets," Marcus mused, his voice echoing in the crystalline space. "And all it took was one glimpse of what he could not bear to see." He looked at Lira, whose hands still hovered over Kael's ashen face. "Step aside, scholar. Your mending arts are useless against a wound woven into time itself."

Lira didn't move. The silver-green light around her fingers intensified, her knuckles white. "You knew what it would cost him. You let him touch it."

"Knowledge requires sacrifice," Marcus said, taking another slow step forward. "His sacrifice illuminated the path. The Heart showed him the future *I* will build—a unified dominion under the Shadow Council. His despair is the proof of its inevitability." His eyes glittered with fervent hunger. "Now, it will show me how to make it so."

Kael, slumped against the ice, let out a ragged breath. The grey was receding from his vision, but the hollow feeling inside was vast and terrifying. He could feel each fragile beat of his heart as if it were a separate, borrowed thing. Lira's magic was a bandage on a bleeding soul. He saw Marcus's obsession, saw the Heart's dark promise reflected in the commander's eyes. It would consume him, too. Not just seconds, but his very reason.

"You will not touch it," Lira whispered, her voice trembling with a strength she didn't feel.

"I will not be denied!" Marcus snarled, lunging past her.
