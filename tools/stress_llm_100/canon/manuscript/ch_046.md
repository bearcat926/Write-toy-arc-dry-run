# Chapter 46: The Echoes We Choose

The silence held the weight of a verdict. Lira’s hand trembled where it hovered over Kael’s shoulder, not from cold, but from the sheer, terrible clarity of what she’d just witnessed. The frost on his skin was a map of temporal agony, and she had been its cartographer.

“The library in Thalassia,” she whispered, her voice raw. “The Vorkosian Manuscript… it spoke of ‘chronal scarring,’ the mark left when a consciousness is forced to perceive the multitudinous stream. It was considered a metaphor.” She finally touched his shoulder, a point of grounding warmth against the spectral chill. “It is not a metaphor.”

Kael pushed himself up, every muscle protesting. The world seemed faintly translucent, the shadows at the glacier’s edges breathing with latent possibility. He focused on Lira’s face—the *real* one—forcing the phantom versions to blur. “The Heart did more than rewind. It… showed me. What was, what could be.” His voice was rough. “I saw you die, Lira. In three different ways.”

The confession hung between them. Lira’s scholar’s mind, which catalogued fates and prophecies, recoiled from the visceral intimacy of that statement. This was no abstract doom. It was Kael’s raw psyche, burned by forbidden sight.

“We thought the cost was memory,” she said, thinking aloud, her fear transmuting into icy analysis. “A price paid in past. But that’s too simple. The Obsidian Heart doesn’t just consume time; it *corrupts* the perception of it. It doesn’t erase, it… layers.” She looked at the healed cuts on his palms, now traced with faint, silver veins. “You’re not just remembering what happened at the Shattered Spire, Kael. You’re remembering all the times it *almost* happened differently. The burden isn’t loss. It’s the unbearable weight of choice.”

Her development crystallized in that moment—the scholar’s detachment shattering, replaced by a fierce, protective insight. The prophecy wasn’t about a weapon. It was about a curse that unraveled the chooser.
