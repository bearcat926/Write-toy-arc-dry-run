# Chapter 19: The Glacial Heartbeat

The *Silver Fin* found no purchase at Nordmark’s port. Instead, Lira’s guided course drew them into a fjord so narrow its icy walls seemed to close over them like a book shutting. The air grew still and impossibly cold, leaching the moisture from their breath and turning the sails to brittle sheets. They moored at a shelf of black rock, a desolate landing before a sheer wall of blue-veined ice.

“The Sorrowing Ravine,” Lira murmured, her breath a silver plume. She tapped the compass; its needle, now a solid shard of frost, pointed directly at the glacier. “The source. It’s behind all this.”

Kael felt it then, not with his ears but in the marrow of his bones—a slow, deep pulse. It was not a heartbeat of life, but of something else: a rhythmic compression of silence. Dawnbreaker, slung across his back, grew perceptibly warmer, as if in uneasy sympathy.

“It knows,” Kael said, the words tasting of frost. The weapon had been restless for days, a low hum in his senses that now resolved into this. The Obsidian Heart was calling to it, or to the light it wielded. Two opposing absolutes.

Lira, her scholar’s curiosity warring with visible fear, traced a line in the frost on a nearby rock. “The texts speak of a ‘glacial lodestone.’ A piece of time itself, locked in perpetual decay and renewal. It doesn’t just manipulate time, Kael. It *weeps* it. The cost… it’s not a payment. It’s erosion.”

He thought of the memory-glass, of Marcus’s eyes stripped of hatred. “What does it erode?”

“Meaning,” she said simply. “Sequence. The ‘why’ that connects one moment to the next. Use it, and you might rewind a death, but you might also unravel the reason for the life.”

The pulsing seemed to strengthen. A fine, crystalline dust sifted down from the ravine entrance. Kael unslung Dawnbreaker; its golden glow cast long, dancing shadows on the ice, illuminating what lay within.

Embedded in the glacier’s heart, a hundred feet in, was a shape of pure, light-absorbing black. Not a stone, but a tear in the very structure of the ice, a place where the laws of reflection failed. Around it, the glacial strata were twisted, banded with veins of frozen silver and blue, all flowing toward that singular point of null.

Kael took a step onto the ice.
