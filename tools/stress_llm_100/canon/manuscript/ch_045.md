# Chapter 45: The Scholar's Burden

The silence on the glacier was worse than the screaming silver had been. Kael knelt in the snow, his breath coming in ragged bursts, the last echoes of temporal displacement fading from his eyes. The frost on his skin had receded, but the map of its pain was etched into his face—lines that hadn’t been there an hour ago.

“The Silent Fen,” Lira said, her voice quiet but absolute. She stood a few paces away, her back to him, consulting a star-chart unfurled on a flat stone. The constellations were wrong, they both knew, but the Fen was marked on all maps, a place where time was said to bleed. It was a suicide destination for most. For them, it was a beacon.

“We can’t,” Kael rasped, pushing himself to his feet. Dawnbreaker felt heavier, its light dimmed. “The cost… you saw what happens. Every time I… it takes something. Not from me. From *here*.” He gestured to the world around them, to the faint shimmer in the air where reality hadn’t quite re-knit itself after his fall.

Lira rolled up the chart, her movements precise. “That is exactly why we must go. The Heart’s resonance is strongest there, a nexus. If we are to understand the prophecy—to understand what it is doing to you—we must go to where its voice is loudest.” She finally turned, and Kael was struck by the change in her. The scholarly fear was still there, but it had been sublimated into something colder, more focused. “You are no longer just carrying a sword, Kael. You are carrying a fracture. I cannot heal what I cannot study.”

Her words hit him with the force of truth. He was the symptom; the Obsidian Heart was the disease
