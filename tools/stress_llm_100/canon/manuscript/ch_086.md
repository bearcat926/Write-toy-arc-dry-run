# Chapter 86: The Fracture’s Price

The fractured Heart pulsed once, a deep, silent throb that pushed Kael’s breath from his lungs. It wasn’t a sound, but a pressure in the mind, a vision of moments flickering—the Fall of Valdris, the signing of the Eryndor Compact, a child laughing in a sunlit courtyard that Kael had never seen. Time itself was leaking from the crack.

“Kael, you must move,” Lira’s voice was a strained whisper. Her staff, usually a calm blue, flickered erratically. “The seal is broken. Not destroyed, but… perforated. It’s seeking an equilibrium by absorbing moments from the fabric around it.”

He could see it. The frozen motes of dust from the earlier vortex now danced in complex, backward spirals. A fragment of wall, crumbling to ruin, would suddenly reassemble, only to decay again in the space of a heartbeat. The Sanctum was unstitching itself.

“The cost…” Kael grunted, pushing himself up. His muscles screamed. Dawnbreaker felt heavier, its light a sickly, fluttering gold. “What is it?”

“Paradox,” Lira breathed, her eyes wide, fixed on the bleeding artifact. “Every moment it borrows, it erases another. Not from history, but from *possibility*. Futures die here, Kael. We are standing in a graveyard of what-never-will-be.”
