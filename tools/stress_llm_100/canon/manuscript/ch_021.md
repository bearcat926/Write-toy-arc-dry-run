# Chapter 21: The Temporal Fracture

The Obsidian Heart did not sit upon a plinth; it hovered, an absence of light in the heart of the glacial chamber. It was the size of a human heart, carved from a single piece of volcanic glass that drank the blue luminescence around it. Veins of deeper black pulsed within its structure, not with light, but with a promise of shadow. Lira, ever the scholar, took a hesitant step forward, her hand outstretched as if to touch a text of immense fragility.

“Don’t.” Kael’s voice was a rasp. Dawnbreaker, slung at his back, felt cold—a sensation it had never granted him before. The artifact wasn't just dark; it *competed* with the sword, creating a tangible void where its light should have filled the room.

“It’s not just an object, Kael. It’s a… a key,” Lira whispered, her eyes reflecting the paradoxical non-light. Her scholar’s awe was warring with something deeper, something primal. “The texts spoke of a core that could unwind cause from effect. A spindle for the thread of time.”

A new sound, then. Not the glacial heartbeat, but the crisp, confident tread of armored boots on ancient ice. From the shadows of the archway they’d entered, a figure emerged, flanked by two soldiers clad in matte-black plate. Marcus. His scarred face was alight with a triumphant, feverish hunger.

“Magnificent, isn’t it?” the Shadow Council commander said, his voice echoing in the vault. He paid Kael no mind, his gaze fixed on the Heart. “To think, the Valdrisian throne wars over grain and borders, while the key to rewriting those borders—to unwriting the kings themselves—has been waiting here, singing its silent song.”

Kael’s hand moved instinctively to Dawnbreaker’s hilt. The sword hummed, but the sound was strained, discordant.
