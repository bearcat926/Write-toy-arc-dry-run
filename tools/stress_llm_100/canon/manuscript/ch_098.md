# Chapter 98: The Tide That Remembers

The Chamber of Echoes lived up to its name. Kael’s accusation seemed to bounce from wall to wall, each return carrying a different shade of meaning: “You knew.” *You knew.* You *knew*.

Lira’s head bowed, not in submission, but in the weary acceptance of a librarian who must finally show a reader the forbidden text. “I did not know the *shape* of the lie,” she said, her voice finding strength. “But I felt its shadow. In the margins of the oldest maps, in the footnotes of chronicles burned long ago. References to the ‘First Oath-Sworn’—a title bestowed not by merit, but by necessity. A clan forged in the blood of a covenant so dark, history chose to forget it rather than record it.” She looked up, her elven eyes, usually pools of calm, swirling with guilt. “I was afraid if I told you before we reached the Heart… you would turn back. That the world would lose its only chance to mend the fracture.”

Kael’s hand tightened on Dawnbreaker’s hilt. The sword’s light pulsed weakly, no longer a confident star, but a dying ember. “A fracture your order helped create by hiding the truth. You scholars of Eryndor, you sift through the ashes of Valdris and Nordmark, picking over our wars, but you never dig for the fire’s origin.”
