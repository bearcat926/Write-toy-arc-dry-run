# Chapter 81: The Echo in the Glass

The Sanctum’s reality groaned, a sound felt in the teeth. Kael’s charge was not a thought but an instinct, honed in a thousand battles where hesitation meant death. Dawnbreaker sang, its golden light cutting a fragile corridor through the ageless decay. He aimed not for Marcus’s obsidian heart, but for the fractured pedestal beneath him—the anchor point of this corruption.

“Kael, no!” Lira’s voice was raw, strained against the temporal pull. She was pressed against a shimmering wall, her fingers tracing frantic runes in the air that sputtered out like damp sparks. “The Heart isn’t just using him—it’s *remembering* him! Every moment he was…!”

Her warning was swallowed by impact. Kael’s blade struck the ancient stone, and instead of shattering it, the pedestal *rippled*. The obsidian growth on Marcus’s chest pulsed once, violently. The light of Dawnbreaker didn’t disperse—it was captured, refracted, and flung back not as a weapon, but as an image.

The air before Kael solidified into a mirror of polished night. In it, he saw not his own reflection, but Marcus. A younger Marcus, clad in Shadow Council leather, his face unmarred by the obsidian and filled with a zealot’s fire. The echo-Marcus raised a hand, and from the true Marcus behind him, a shard of pure temporal energy lanced forward.

Kael threw himself aside, the shard aging the stone where he’d stood to dust in a heartbeat. He rolled, coming up to one knee, his mind reeling. The Heart wasn’t just manipulating time; it was weaponizing *memory*, giving the past a savage, present-moment agency.

“The echoes… it’s using his own timeline against us!” Lira shouted, her scholarly mind finally piercing the chaos. She wasn’t just afraid; she was *calculating*, her eyes darting between the pulsing Heart and the memory-mirror. “It’s a closed loop! The power feeds on his past choices to fuel his present reign!”

The true Marcus hadn’t moved, but a slow, cruel smile etched itself across his new obsidian face. The mirror shimmered, and a different echo appeared—this one older, bearded, with a look of profound weariness and regret. It was a future Marcus, perhaps, or a potential one. This echo looked directly at Kael, and though no sound came from its lips, Kael understood the silent message in its eyes: *It is too late*.
