# Chapter 90: The Commander’s Gambit

The scent of ozone and ancient dust hung heavy in the Chamber of Echoes. Kael’s eyes, still seared with the afterimage of Valdris turning to sand, focused on the Obsidian Heart. It pulsed now with a arrhythmic, sickly rhythm, its fractured surface weeping a slow, dark light that pooled on the stone like liquid shadow. Each pulse didn’t just push time; it *ate* it. The candles at the chamber’s edge had burned down to nubs in what felt like a single breath, then flickered back to half-length.

“Kael, listen to me.” Lira’s voice was a strained whisper. She had one hand outstretched toward the artifact, not to touch it, but to feel the chaotic eddies of temporal energy, the other pressed against the chanting script on the wall as if for balance. “The Makers didn’t create a weapon. They were trying to *suture* a wound in time itself. A natural rift. This… this is the suture snapping.”

“It’s hurting,” Kael grunted, wiping a fresh trickle of blood from his nose with the back of his gauntlet. Dawnbreaker felt heavy and cold at his hip, its holy light subdued, cowed by the Heart’s pervasive wrongness. “The world… I can feel it screaming. Every moment that was, trying to be *now*.”

He saw it then, not as a vision, but as a physical manifestation. A spectral legion of Valdrisian knights marched through the far wall, their forms translucent, their silent shouts lost to their own time. They passed through Lira without her noticing, their purpose long spent. The fractures were becoming permanent scars in the air.

“We must seal it again,” Lira said, her scholar’s mind racing. “Not with force. Dawnbreaker is a blade of the present—it fights change. We need a moment. A perfect, complete moment to act as a new keystone.”
