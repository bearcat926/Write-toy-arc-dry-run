# Chapter 57: The Unravelling Thread

The glacier’s death was a silent, violent sigh. The floor heaved. Pillars of ice, ancient and pale blue, snapped with sounds like the breaking of giant bones. Kael was thrown from the dais, his shoulder slamming into the fractured stone. Dawnbreaker sang a warning note, its opalescent light cutting frantic arcs through the billowing mist of sublimating ice.

But the cold was the least of it. The moment his skin had touched the Obsidian Heart’s surface, a shard of frozen eternity had lodged itself behind his eyes. Now, the world fractured further.

*He saw Lira, not falling, but already on her knees, her mouth open in a scream he couldn’t hear. Her face was lined, her silver hair streaked white. Then it was smooth again, a young elf clinging to a book in a burning library.*

*He saw the glacier whole, above a green valley, under a red sun. He saw it as dust, drifting over a desert.*

*He saw Marcus, his Shadow Council robes not black, but a brilliant, sun-gold. He stood in a hall of marble, laughing with a man whose face was Kael’s own.*

The visions were not sequential. They were simultaneous, a cacophony of moments past, possible, and stolen, all pressing into a single, screaming instant. He felt time like a physical current, trying to pull him apart into all the selves he had been, could be, or never would be.

“Kael!” Lira’s voice cut through, real and present. She was at his side, her hand gripping his arm, her own face pale with a terror beyond the collapsing mountain. She wasn’t looking at the chaos around them. She was looking at his eyes. “Your eyes… they’re… flickering.”
