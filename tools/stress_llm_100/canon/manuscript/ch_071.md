# Chapter 71: The Unraveling Thread

The defiance was a foreign taste in his mouth, metallic and sharp like blood. Kael held Dawnbreaker between himself and the Obsidian Heart, the blade’s golden light sputtering against the artifact’s hungry, silent glow. The Sanctum was no longer merely silent; it held its breath.

Then, the whisper came again, not from the shard this time, but from the Heart itself. It slithered through the air, a soundless vibration that translated into meaning directly in his mind. *You see only the cost. You cannot see the gain.*

Lira, who had been pressed against a wall, her scholar’s eyes wide with terror and awe, suddenly gasped. “Kael! The pillars! The light!”

He risked a glance. The veins of light snaking up the cavern walls, which had pulsed in time with the shard’s commands, were now racing erratically. They converged not on the Heart, but on the space behind Kael, the path he had walked. The air there shimmered, not with heat, but with a distortion, a warping of space itself. The silence was broken by a faint, distant sound: the crunch of gravel under a boot that hadn’t made the sound seconds ago.

“What’s happening?” Kael’s voice was raw.

“It’s… showing me,” Lira breathed, her fingers tracing the air. “Not knowledge. A memory. Your refusal. It’s… *unthreading* the moment you chose it. Trying to show you a different path.” Her face paled. “Kael, it’s using time to *seduce* you.”
