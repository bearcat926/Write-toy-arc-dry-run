# Chapter 27: The Sun's Last Grace

Kael’s world had narrowed to the grey edge of his own vision and the fierce, steady pressure of Lira’s hands on his temples. The elven light she channeled felt like cool water poured over a dying ember—soothing, but unable to reignite what was gone. He saw the strain in the tight lines around her mouth, the dilated focus of her silver eyes.

“It is… older than the Breaking,” she whispered, more to herself than to him. “The cost isn’t just time. It’s a borrow against everything you are. I can’t mend a thread that’s been unraveled from your tapestry.”

A tremor shook the glacial chamber. Not from the Heart, which pulsed silently on its pedestal, but from the tunnel behind them. The rhythmic, grinding crack of advancing ice-breakers. Marcus. They were minutes away.
