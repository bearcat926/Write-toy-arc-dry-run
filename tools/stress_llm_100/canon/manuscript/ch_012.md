# Chapter 12: The Cartographer's Lament

The map did not just whisper; it wept.

Lira’s fingers hovered over the carvings, her breath held. The low, sorrowful murmur that had guided her here resolved into words, not of instruction, but of warning. “They sealed it away… not to hide it, but to mourn it. The Heart does not turn back time. It unravels it, weaving present and past into a tapestry where both threads fray.”

Kael stood behind her, the weight of Dawnbreaker a familiar burden. The unease he’d carried since the tunnel’s collapse had sharpened, focusing on the map’s central symbol—a spiraling vortex of obsidian. “Where does it weep from?” he asked, his voice rough.

“Here.” Lira tapped the spiraling vortex. The stone beneath was colder than the rest. “The coordinates are old… older than Valdris’s founding. It points to the Shattered Peaks, in the disputed borderlands between Eryndor and Nordmark.” She turned, her violet eyes holding a scholar’s fierce clarity. “This is a mnemonic of grief, Kael. Whoever carved this wasn’t just mapping a location; they were marking a tragedy.”

He remembered the ash, the void where Marcus had been. The terrible cost was becoming less abstract. “We go to the Peaks.”

As if in answer, Dawnbreaker pulsed once—not with light, but with a deep, resonant chill that sank into his bones. The torchlight on the wall flickered, and for a heartbeat, the shadows cast by the whorl seemed to swirl with fleeting images: a city of white towers dissolving into sand, a battlefield where warriors moved in reverse, their wounds closing as they rose from the dead. A premonition, or a memory?

Lira gasped, pointing. “Your hand!”
