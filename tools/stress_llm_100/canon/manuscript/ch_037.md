# Chapter 37: The Shattered Lens

The cold was no longer external; it lived in the marrow of Kael’s bones. He sat by the low fire Lira had coaxed from damp wood, her unsuccessful healing a hollow memory. His gaze was fixed on the frost patterns creeping across the tent’s inner canvas, but he was not truly seeing them. He was listening to the echoes.

Not the wails of the time-lost, but the quiet, insistent whispers of his own timeline, now fractured. He could feel Lira’s worried breath, seconds before it stirred the air. He sensed the precise moment a snowflake would land on the tent peak, a half-second before the soft *tick*. It was disorienting, nauseating, a chorus of almost-nows.

“Kael.” Lira’s voice was soft, yet it came to him in a double-reverberation—the sound she made, and the sound he perceived her making a heartbeat later. “You’re shivering.”

He was? He looked at his hands. Steady. Still. But a tremor ran through him, a violent, internal shudder that had nothing to do with temperature. “I can hear the seams,” he murmured, his voice rough. “The moments… they’re all laid bare. Like glass cracked but not yet fallen.”

Lira knelt before him, her elven eyes searching his face. “The Obsidian Heart’s touch. It didn’t just take time from the glacier; it unraveled your connection to the flow. You’re perceiving the strands.”

“It’s a cacophony.” He closed his eyes, but the echoes only grew louder behind his lids—the sound of Lira’s heart beating, the scrape of a pebble shifting under tent fabric, the distant, fractional snap of a twig under a boot that hadn’t fallen yet. He flinched from the last one, his eyes flying open.

“Someone’s coming.”

Lira’s hand went to the dagger at her belt, her body taut. “Marcus?”

Kael shook his head, trying to isolate the echo, to pin it down. “The boot… heavy. Military tread. But the echo is… wrong. It’s shadowed. Twisted.” He pressed his palms to his temples. “It’s not just a future step. It feels… borrowed.”
