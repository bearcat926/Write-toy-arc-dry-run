# Chapter 44: The Unravelled Now

The frost burned into Kael’s vision, painting the world in trails of light. He wasn’t just seeing Lira; he was seeing her *possibilities*. A Lira with silver hair and haunted eyes, clutching a book in ruins. A Lira laughing in a sun-dappled courtyard, unaware of his gaze. A Lira weeping over a body he couldn’t see. The present Lira—the one whose hands gripped his shoulders, her face pale with worry—was just one thread in a blinding, terrible tapestry.

“Kael, look at me. *This* me,” she commanded, her voice cutting through the symphony of whispers that were other versions of her own. The scholarly intensity was gone, replaced by raw fear. For him.

He focused, gritting his teeth against the internal fire. “It’s… leaking,” he rasped. “Time. I see your… threads.”

Lira’s breath caught. “The Heart. You touched its echo, or it touched you. The temporal frost isn’t a mark. It’s a lens.” Her fingers traced a particularly intricate pattern on his wrist, not cold now, but humming with latent energy. “You’re seeing the Vein of Hours in a living being. The paths not taken.”

A path not taken. The phrase struck him with physical force. What path had led him here, to this freezing peak, to this woman whose life was now a kaleidoscope before his eyes? Was it the warrior’s path, or the artifact’s?

He pushed himself up, the effort sending a fresh wave of visions—Marcus’s face, older and crueler, seated on a throne of shadows. He staggered. “It’s not just you. The glacier… the Shadow Council… everything is fraying.”

“Then we must bind it,” Lira said, her scholar’s mind seizing on a solution even as her heart hammered against her ribs. She pulled the wrapped shard of the Obsidian Heart from her pack.
