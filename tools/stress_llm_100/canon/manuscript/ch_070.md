# Chapter 70: The Price of Refusal

The Heart did not like being denied.

A subsonic vibration thrummed through the Sanctum’s stone, traveling up through Kael’s boots and into his bones. The veins of light in the obsidian surface flickered erratically, like a dying pulse. The air, once heavy with ancient power, grew thin and sharp, tasting of ozone and regret.

“No,” Kael growled, the word ripped from a place deeper than his throat. He clenched his fist, the leather of his gauntlet creaking. Dawnbreaker’s light, held firmly at his side, seemed to shrink back from the unstable artifact. The knowledge in his blood, that insistent map of moments, began to fracture. Glimpses—of this very Sanctum in pristine glory, of robed figures channeling light into the Heart, of the same figures turning to dust as reality buckled—jittered behind his eyes.

“It’s destabilizing!” Lira’s voice cut through the psychic noise, sharp with alarm. She stood a dozen paces away, her own hand pressed to her temple. “The temporal field around it is becoming unmoored. Kael, your resistance is like a stone thrown into a pond—it’s creating ripples backwards and forwards.”

“Then let it ripple!” Kael snarled, taking a deliberate step back. The movement felt like tearing muscle. “I will not be its vessel.
