# Chapter 96: The Heart's Remonstrance

The silence that followed Kael’s accusation was a living thing, coiling through the Chamber of Echoes. Lira did not flinch, but the ancient parchment on her lap curled at the edges, as if desiccated by the truth laid bare. The light of Dawnbreaker in Kael’s hand guttered, not from lack of power, but from a dissonance that now plagued its wielder.

“I knew a legend,” Lira whispered, her scholar’s composure cracking. “A conjecture pieced from fragments. Not this… visceral certainty.” Her eyes, old with elven memory, held his. “I needed you to reach the Heart. Your lineage was the key I could not risk telling you. The prophecy binds those of shadow-blood to its unmaking.”

From the far archway, Marcus let out a dry chuckle. “She played you, Sunstrider. Or should I say, *Shadowborn*? The elves and their games of omission. They always did prefer their lies dressed in scholarly silks.”

Kael’s grip tightened on Dawnbreaker’s hilt. The weapon felt alien, a torch in a house he now knew was built over a tomb. He looked from Lira’s guilt-stricken face to Marcus’s triumphant sneer. The old enemy was right. And that was the most bitter pill of all.

“Why?” The word was torn from Kael, aimed at both of them. “Why build a clan on such a lie? Why sanctify us with a sun-sword if our very blood was consecrated to shadow?”

Marcus stepped forward, the shadows clinging to his armor seeming to deepen. “Because the Heart requires balance, boy. Light to call it forth, shadow to bind it. The Valdris founders weren’t saints; they were desperate. They created the Sunstriders as living locks for the Obsidian Heart. And you… you were always meant to be the key to its final cage.”
