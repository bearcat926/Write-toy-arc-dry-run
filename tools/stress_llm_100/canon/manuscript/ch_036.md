# Chapter 36: The Frost That Follows

Lira’s hands glowed with a soft, golden light, the healing energy of the ancient Sylvan texts. She held them over Kael’s temples, her brow furrowed in concentration, but the light seemed to sink into his skin and vanish, like water on parched earth.

“It’s not an injury I can mend,” she whispered, her voice tight with worry. “It’s… an absence. A hollow where something vital was scooped out.”

Kael sat on a low stone bench in the relative shelter of the chamber’s entrance archway. The glacier was gone, but the cold remained, a deep, clammy chill that emanated from within him. He could still feel the echoes—not of the Heart’s stolen moments, but of his own self being pulled apart. The man who had returned was a fragment.

“I can feel it, Lira,” he said, his voice hoarse. “Time. It’s… lagging. Like the world is moving through syrup for me.” He watched a snowflake drift down, its descent taking an eternity. When it finally landed on his hand, it didn’t melt. It simply sat there, perfectly formed, a tiny crystal of frozen time.

Lira’s elven sight was sharper. “Your aura. It’s… distorted. Flickering. You stepped outside the flow, Kael. And you brought some of that outside back with you.”

A sound echoed from the lower levels of the ruins—a scraping, dragging noise. They both tensed. Kael’s hand went to his hip, finding only empty air where Dawnbreaker’s hilt should have been. The loss was a fresh stab of grief.

“Marcus?” Lira breathed, nocking an arrow to her bow.

Kael shook his head slowly, his eyes fixed on the dark stairwell. “No. Something else.”

From the shadows emerged a figure, humanoid but wrong. It was a man, or had been, clad in the tattered fur and leather of a Nordmark scout. His movements were jerky, skipping frames like a poorly drawn animation. One moment he was at the base of the stairs, the next he was halfway across the floor, his foot still raised from a step that had happened seconds before. His eyes were wide, milky, and utterly vacant, staring at nothing. Frost covered his armor in intricate, unnatural patterns.

Lira lowered her bow, horrified. “By the High Boughs… he’s caught in a loop. A sliver of time, broken and replaying.”

The scout’s head snapped towards Kael, a grinding motion that should have been impossible. His frozen lips moved, and a voice emerged, a chorus of whispers speaking in unison, in Kael’s own tone: *“The cost… is memory… the cost… is memory…”*
